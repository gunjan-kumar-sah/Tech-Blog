# 📖 What are Grammatical Errors?

**Grammatical errors** are mistakes in sentence structure that make writing or speaking incorrect or unclear.

They may involve:

* Subject–Verb Agreement
* Tenses
* Pronouns
* Articles
* Prepositions
* Word Order
* Punctuation
* Sentence Structure

# 🧩 Types of Common Grammar Errors

| Error Type             | Example                                      |
| ---------------------- | -------------------------------------------- |
| Subject–Verb Agreement | He *go* → He **goes**                        |
| Tense                  | I *am went* → I **went**                     |
| Pronoun                | Me is ready → I am ready                     |
| Article                | He is engineer → He is **an** engineer       |
| Preposition            | Discuss about → Discuss                      |
| Word Order             | Always I go → I always go                    |
| Double Negative        | I don't know nothing → I don't know anything |

# 🟢 Subject–Verb Agreement Errors

The verb must agree with the subject in **number** and **person**.

---

## Incorrect

❌ He go to school.

## Correct

✅ He goes to school.

---

## Incorrect

❌ They plays cricket.

## Correct

✅ They play cricket.

---

## Incorrect

❌ The students was present.

## Correct

✅ The students were present.

---

# 🔵 Tense Errors

Choose the correct tense according to the time of action.

---

## Incorrect

❌ Yesterday I go to college.

## Correct

✅ Yesterday I went to college.

---

## Incorrect

❌ She has completed the work yesterday.

## Correct

✅ She completed the work yesterday.

---

## Incorrect

❌ I am knowing the answer.

## Correct

✅ I know the answer.

---

# 🟣 Pronoun Errors

Pronouns must correctly refer to the subject.

---

## Incorrect

❌ Me and Rahul are friends.

## Correct

✅ Rahul and I are friends.

---

## Incorrect

❌ Him is my brother.

## Correct

✅ He is my brother.

---

## Incorrect

❌ Everyone should bring their book. *(Formal Grammar)*

## Correct

✅ Everyone should bring his or her book.

> **Note:** In modern English, **their** is also widely accepted as a singular, gender-neutral pronoun in many contexts.

# 🟡 Basic Grammar Correction Rules

### Rule 1

Match the subject with the correct verb.

---

### Rule 2

Use the correct tense for time expressions.

---

### Rule 3

Choose the correct pronoun.

---

### Rule 4

Avoid unnecessary words.

❌ Discuss about

✅ Discuss

---

### Rule 5

Use only one negative word.

❌ I don't know nothing.

✅ I don't know anything.

# 🌍 Everyday Examples

### Subject–Verb

❌ She write well.

✅ She writes well.

---

### Tense

❌ I have seen him yesterday.

✅ I saw him yesterday.

---

### Pronoun

❌ Him and me went there.

✅ He and I went there.

---

### Word Order

❌ Always I wake up early.

✅ I always wake up early.

---

### Article

❌ He bought umbrella.

✅ He bought an umbrella.

# ⚠️ Common Mistakes

### Mistake 1

❌ One of the students are absent.

✅ One of the students is absent.

---

### Mistake 2

❌ Neither of the boys are ready.

✅ Neither of the boys is ready.

---

### Mistake 3

❌ Each students has a notebook.

✅ Each student has a notebook.

---

### Mistake 4

❌ She don't like tea.

✅ She doesn't like tea.

---

### Mistake 5

❌ I am agree.

✅ I agree.
