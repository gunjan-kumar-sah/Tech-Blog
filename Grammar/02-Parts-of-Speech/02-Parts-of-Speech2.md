# 📖 What are Parts of Speech?

**Parts of Speech** are the different categories of words based on **their role and function in a sentence**.

Every English word belongs to one of these categories.

Understanding Parts of Speech helps you:

* Write grammatically correct sentences.
* Improve spoken English.
* Solve placement aptitude questions.
* Understand sentence structure.
* Avoid common grammar mistakes.

---

# 🌍 Why Learn Parts of Speech?

Learning Parts of Speech is the foundation of English Grammar because they help you:

* 📝 Form correct sentences
* 🗣️ Speak confidently
* 📚 Improve writing skills
* 💼 Perform better in interviews
* 🎯 Crack verbal aptitude sections

# 🧩 The Eight Parts of Speech

English Grammar has **8 Parts of Speech**:

| No. | Part of Speech | Purpose                                        |
| :-: | -------------- | ---------------------------------------------- |
|  1  | Noun           | Names a person, place, thing, or idea          |
|  2  | Pronoun        | Replaces a noun                                |
|  3  | Verb           | Shows an action or state                       |
|  4  | Adjective      | Describes a noun or pronoun                    |
|  5  | Adverb         | Describes a verb, adjective, or another adverb |
|  6  | Preposition    | Shows relationship between words               |
|  7  | Conjunction    | Joins words, phrases, or clauses               |
|  8  | Interjection   | Expresses sudden emotion                       |

# 📚 Overview of Each Part

## 1️⃣ Noun

A **Noun** is the name of a person, place, thing, or idea.

### Examples

* Rahul
* India
* Computer
* School
* Honesty

**Sentence:**

> Rahul is studying.

Here, **Rahul** is a noun.

---

## 2️⃣ Pronoun

A **Pronoun** replaces a noun to avoid repetition.

### Examples

* He
* She
* It
* They
* We

**Sentence:**

> Rahul is studying. **He** is preparing for placements.

Here, **He** replaces **Rahul**.

---

## 3️⃣ Verb

A **Verb** tells us what the subject **does** or **is**.

### Examples

* Run
* Eat
* Write
* Read
* Is

**Sentence:**

> She writes notes every day.

The word **writes** is the verb.

---

## 4️⃣ Adjective

An **Adjective** describes a noun or pronoun.

### Examples

* Beautiful
* Tall
* Smart
* Honest
* Intelligent

**Sentence:**

> She is a **smart** student.

The word **smart** describes the noun **student**.

---

## 5️⃣ Adverb

An **Adverb** describes:

* A Verb
* An Adjective
* Another Adverb

### Examples

* Quickly
* Slowly
* Carefully
* Very
* Extremely

**Sentence:**

> She runs **quickly**.

The word **quickly** tells us **how** she runs.

---

## 🧠 Memory Trick

Remember this simple sequence:

```text
Noun
   ↓
Pronoun replaces Noun

Verb = Action

Adjective describes Noun

Adverb describes Verb
```

This trick helps you remember the first five Parts of Speech easily.

# 🌍 Everyday Example

Sentence:

> **The intelligent boy runs quickly.**

| Word        | Part of Speech |
| ----------- | -------------- |
| The         | Article        |
| Intelligent | Adjective      |
| Boy         | Noun           |
| Runs        | Verb           |
| Quickly     | Adverb         |

---

# 💡 Quick Tips

* **Noun** → Name
* **Pronoun** → Replacement
* **Verb** → Action
* **Adjective** → Description of a noun
* **Adverb** → Description of an action

---

# ⚠️ Common Beginner Mistakes

* Confusing adjectives with adverbs.
* Thinking every action word is the only type of verb.
* Using pronouns without a clear noun.
* Forgetting that some words can change their role depending on the sentence.
