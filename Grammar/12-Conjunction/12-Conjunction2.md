# 2️⃣ Subordinating Conjunctions

## 📖 Definition

A **Subordinating Conjunction** joins an **independent clause** with a **dependent clause**.

The dependent clause cannot express a complete thought on its own.

---

# 🎯 Common Subordinating Conjunctions

* Because
* Although
* Though
* Even though
* Since
* As
* If
* Unless
* Until
* Before
* After
* While
* When
* Whenever
* Where
* Wherever

---

# 📚 Because (Reason)

Use **because** to show the reason for something.

### Examples

✅ She stayed home **because** she was sick.

✅ I worked hard **because** I wanted to succeed.

---

# 📚 Although / Though (Contrast)

Use **although** or **though** to show contrast.

### Examples

✅ Although he is young, he is very responsible.

✅ Though it was raining, they continued playing.

---

# 📚 If (Condition)

Use **if** to express a condition.

### Examples

✅ If you study regularly, you will pass.

✅ If it rains, we will stay home.

---

# 📚 Unless (Negative Condition)

"Unless" means **if not**.

### Examples

✅ You cannot enter unless you have an ID card.

✅ I won't go unless you come with me.

---

# 📚 Before & After (Time)

### Before

✅ Finish your homework before dinner.

### After

✅ We went for a walk after lunch.

---

# 📚 While & When

### While

Two actions happening at the same time.

✅ She listened to music while studying.

---

### When

One action happens at a particular time.

✅ Call me when you reach home.

---

# 📚 Since & As

Both can show **reason** or **time**, depending on the sentence.

### Examples

✅ Since you are here, let's begin.

✅ As it was getting late, we left.

---

# 📊 Subordinating Conjunction Summary

| Conjunction | Purpose              | Example             |
| ----------- | -------------------- | ------------------- |
| Because     | Reason               | Because I was tired |
| Although    | Contrast             | Although it rained  |
| If          | Condition            | If you work hard    |
| Unless      | Negative condition   | Unless you try      |
| Before      | Earlier time         | Before class        |
| After       | Later time           | After dinner        |
| While       | Simultaneous actions | While reading       |
| When        | Specific time        | When I arrived      |

# 3️⃣ Correlative Conjunctions

## 📖 Definition

Correlative conjunctions always work **in pairs** to connect equal words, phrases, or clauses.

---

# Common Correlative Conjunctions

* Either...or
* Neither...nor
* Both...and
* Not only...but also
* Whether...or
* No sooner...than
* Hardly...when

---

# 📚 Either...Or

Shows a choice between two options.

### Examples

✅ Either Rahul or Aman will lead the team.

✅ You can either call me or send a message.

---

# 📚 Neither...Nor

Shows that both options are negative.

### Examples

✅ Neither the teacher nor the students were absent.

✅ Neither coffee nor tea is available.

---

# 📚 Both...And

Joins two positive ideas.

### Examples

✅ Both English and Mathematics are important.

✅ She is both intelligent and hardworking.

---

# 📚 Not Only...But Also

Adds emphasis to two related ideas.

### Examples

✅ He is not only talented but also disciplined.

✅ She not only sings but also dances beautifully.

---

# 📚 Whether...Or

Shows alternatives or possibilities.

### Examples

✅ I don't know whether he will come or not.

✅ Whether you agree or disagree, respect others.

---

# ⚖️ Parallelism Rule

When using correlative conjunctions, keep the sentence structure parallel.

### Correct

✅ She likes **reading and writing**.

✅ He is **honest and hardworking**.

---

### Incorrect

❌ She likes reading and to write.

---

# Corrected

✅ She likes reading and writing.

---

# ⚠️ Common Errors

## Error 1

❌ Although but he tried, he failed.

✅ Although he tried, he failed.

OR

✅ He tried, but he failed.

---

## Error 2

❌ Because so I was late.

✅ Because I was late...

OR

✅ I was late, so...

---

## Error 3

❌ Either Rahul and Aman will come.

✅ Either Rahul or Aman will come.

---

## Error 4

❌ Neither he or his friend attended.

✅ Neither he nor his friend attended.

---

## Error 5

❌ Not only he is smart but also honest.

✅ He is not only smart but also honest.
