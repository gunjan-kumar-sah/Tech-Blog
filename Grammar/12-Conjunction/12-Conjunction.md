# 📖 What is a Conjunction?

A **Conjunction** is a word that **joins**:

* Words
* Phrases
* Clauses
* Sentences

It helps make communication smoother and more meaningful.

---

# 🌍 Examples

### Example 1

> Rahul **and** Aman are friends.

**And** joins two nouns.

---

### Example 2

> I studied hard, **but** I failed.

**But** joins two independent clauses.

---

### Example 3

> Work hard **or** you will fail.

**Or** shows a choice.

---

### Example 4

> She stayed home **because** it was raining.

**Because** gives a reason.

---

# 🎯 Why Are Conjunctions Important?

Conjunctions help you:

* Connect ideas logically
* Write better essays and emails
* Improve spoken English
* Solve grammar questions
* Build professional communication skills

# 1️⃣ Coordinating Conjunctions

## 📖 Definition

Coordinating conjunctions join:

* Two words
* Two phrases
* Two independent clauses

Both parts have equal importance.

---

# 🎯 FANBOYS Rule

The easiest way to remember coordinating conjunctions is:

```text id="fanboys"

F → For

A → And

N → Nor

B → But

O → Or

Y → Yet

S → So

```

---

# 📚 FOR

Shows **reason**.

Example:

✅ I stayed home, **for** I was tired.

---

# 📚 AND

Shows **addition**.

Examples:

✅ Rahul and Aman are classmates.

✅ She sings and dances.

---

# 📚 NOR

Shows **negative alternatives**.

Examples:

✅ He does not drink, nor does he smoke.

---

# 📚 BUT

Shows **contrast**.

Examples:

✅ She is rich, but she is humble.

✅ I tried hard, but I couldn't win.

---

# 📚 OR

Shows **choice**.

Examples:

✅ Tea or coffee?

✅ Hurry up, or you'll miss the bus.

---

# 📚 YET

Shows **unexpected contrast**.

Examples:

✅ He is poor, yet he is happy.

---

# 📚 SO

Shows **result**.

Examples:

✅ It was raining, so we stayed inside.

---

# 📊 FANBOYS Summary Table

| Conjunction | Function            | Example                    |
| ----------- | ------------------- | -------------------------- |
| For         | Reason              | I stayed, for I was tired. |
| And         | Addition            | Rahul and Aman             |
| Nor         | Negative            | Nor did he speak.          |
| But         | Contrast            | Poor but honest            |
| Or          | Choice              | Tea or Coffee              |
| Yet         | Unexpected contrast | Poor yet happy             |
| So          | Result              | It rained, so we left      |

# 📝 Sentence Joining Examples

### Example 1

Sentence 1:

Rahul is smart.

Sentence 2:

Rahul is hardworking.

Joined:

✅ Rahul is smart **and** hardworking.

---

### Example 2

Sentence 1:

He studied.

Sentence 2:

He failed.

Joined:

✅ He studied hard, **but** he failed.

---

### Example 3

Sentence 1:

Leave now.

Sentence 2:

You will miss the train.

Joined:

✅ Leave now, **or** you will miss the train.

---

# 📌 Rules for Coordinating Conjunctions

## Rule 1

Use a comma before a coordinating conjunction when it joins two independent clauses.

✅ She studied hard, **but** she failed.

---

## Rule 2

Do not use a comma when joining only words or short phrases.

✅ Apples and oranges are healthy.

---

## Rule 3

Use **and** to add similar ideas.

✅ She is intelligent and confident.

---

## Rule 4

Use **but** to show contrast.

✅ He is old but energetic.

---

# ⚠️ Common Mistakes

### Mistake 1

❌ Although but he was tired, he worked.

✅ Although he was tired, he worked.

OR

✅ He was tired, but he worked.

---

### Mistake 2

❌ Because so I was late.

✅ Because I was late...

OR

✅ I was late, so...

---

### Mistake 3

❌ And also

✅ And

OR

✅ Also

(Do not use both together unnecessarily.)
