# ➡️ 3. Prepositions of Direction

## 📖 Definition

Prepositions of Direction show **where someone or something is going**.

They answer the question:

> **Where to?**

---

## ⭐ Common Direction Prepositions

* To
* Into
* Onto
* Toward / Towards
* From
* Through
* Across
* Along
* Up
* Down

---

# 📚 Rule 1: **To**

Use **to** when moving toward a destination.

### Examples

✅ I am going **to** school.

✅ She went **to** the market.

✅ They travelled **to** Mumbai.

---

# 📚 Rule 2: **Into**

Use **into** when moving **from outside to inside**.

### Examples

✅ He walked **into** the room.

✅ The cat jumped **into** the box.

✅ She poured water **into** the glass.

---

### ⚠️ Difference: **In** vs **Into**

| In                           | Into                           |
| ---------------------------- | ------------------------------ |
| Shows position               | Shows movement                 |
| The keys are **in** the bag. | Put the keys **into** the bag. |

---

# 📚 Rule 3: **Onto**

Use **onto** when something moves **to the top of a surface**.

### Examples

✅ The child climbed **onto** the bed.

✅ The bird flew **onto** the roof.

---

### ⚠️ Difference: **On** vs **Onto**

| On                            | Onto                             |
| ----------------------------- | -------------------------------- |
| Position                      | Movement                         |
| The book is **on** the table. | Put the book **onto** the table. |

---

# 📚 Rule 4: **Towards**

Shows movement in the direction of something.

### Examples

✅ She walked **towards** the station.

✅ The dog ran **towards** me.

---

# 📚 Rule 5: **From**

Shows the starting point.

### Examples

✅ He came **from** Delhi.

✅ This gift is **from** my friend.

---

# 🚶 4. Prepositions of Movement

These describe movement from one place to another.

---

## Common Movement Prepositions

* Across
* Through
* Along
* Around
* Over
* Under
* Past
* Across
* Up
* Down

---

# 📚 Rule 1: **Across**

Movement from one side to another.

### Examples

✅ They walked **across** the road.

✅ She swam **across** the river.

---

# 📚 Rule 2: **Through**

Movement inside something.

### Examples

✅ The train passed **through** the tunnel.

✅ We walked **through** the forest.

---

### ⚠️ Across vs Through

| Across             | Through            |
| ------------------ | ------------------ |
| Crossing a surface | Passing inside     |
| Across the bridge  | Through the tunnel |

---

# 📚 Rule 3: **Along**

Movement beside something.

### Examples

✅ We walked **along** the beach.

✅ He ran **along** the road.

---

# 📚 Rule 4: **Around**

Movement surrounding something.

### Examples

✅ They walked **around** the park.

✅ The Earth moves **around** the Sun.

---

# 📚 Rule 5: **Over** & **Under**

### Over

Above something without touching.

✅ The airplane flew **over** the city.

---

### Under

Below something.

✅ The dog is sleeping **under** the table.

---

# 🧩 5. Compound Prepositions

## 📖 Definition

Compound prepositions are formed by combining two or more words.

---

### Common Compound Prepositions

* According to
* Because of
* Due to
* Instead of
* In front of
* Next to
* Apart from
* Along with
* Out of

---

# Examples

✅ According to the report, sales increased.

✅ He was absent because of illness.

✅ She sat next to her friend.

✅ They arrived in front of the gate.

---

# 📚 6. Phrase Prepositions

Phrase prepositions are groups of words acting as one preposition.

---

### Common Phrase Prepositions

* In spite of
* On behalf of
* In addition to
* With regard to
* In accordance with
* In place of

---

# Examples

✅ In spite of the rain, we played.

✅ She spoke on behalf of the team.

✅ In addition to English, he knows Hindi.

---

# ⚠️ Confusing Prepositions

## Between vs Among

### Between

Used for **two** people or things.

✅ Divide the money between Rahul and Aman.

---

### Among

Used for **more than two**.

✅ She distributed chocolates among the students.

---

## Beside vs Besides

### Beside

Means **next to**.

✅ Sit beside me.

---

### Besides

Means **in addition to**.

✅ Besides English, I know Python.

---

## Since vs For

### Since

Starting point.

✅ I have lived here since 2022.

---

### For

Duration.

✅ I have lived here for three years.
