---

# 6️⃣ Interrogative Adverbs

## 📖 Definition

Interrogative adverbs are used to ask questions about **time, place, reason, or manner**.

### Common Interrogative Adverbs

* **When** → Time
* **Where** → Place
* **Why** → Reason
* **How** → Manner

---

# Examples

### When

✅ When will you arrive?

---

### Where

✅ Where do you live?

---

### Why

✅ Why are you late?

---

### How

✅ How did you solve the problem?

---

# 7️⃣ Relative Adverbs

## 📖 Definition

Relative adverbs connect two clauses and refer to a previously mentioned noun.

### Common Relative Adverbs

* **Where**
* **When**
* **Why**

---

# Examples

✅ This is the place **where** I was born.

✅ I remember the day **when** we first met.

✅ Do you know the reason **why** he left?

---

# 📍 Position of Adverbs

The position of an adverb depends on its type.

---

## Rule 1: Adverb of Manner

Usually comes **after the main verb** or **after the object**.

Examples:

✅ She sings beautifully.

✅ He completed the task carefully.

---

## Rule 2: Adverb of Place

Usually comes **after the verb or object**.

Examples:

✅ Please sit here.

✅ They looked everywhere.

---

## Rule 3: Adverb of Time

Usually comes at the **beginning or end** of the sentence.

Examples:

✅ Tomorrow we will travel.

✅ We will travel tomorrow.

---

## Rule 4: Adverb of Frequency

Usually comes **before the main verb**.

Examples:

✅ I always drink coffee.

✅ They often visit us.

With helping verbs:

✅ She is always smiling.

---

# ⚖️ Adjective vs Adverb

Many learners confuse adjectives and adverbs.

| Adjective                | Adverb                                 |
| ------------------------ | -------------------------------------- |
| Describes a noun/pronoun | Describes a verb, adjective, or adverb |
| Beautiful girl           | Sings beautifully                      |
| Fast car                 | Runs fast                              |
| Careful student          | Works carefully                        |

---

# Examples

### Adjective

✅ She is a careful driver.

(Careful describes the noun "driver")

---

### Adverb

✅ She drives carefully.

(Carefully describes the verb "drives")

---

# 🔥 Degrees of Comparison of Adverbs

Like adjectives, adverbs also have three degrees.

---

## Positive Degree

Examples:

* Fast
* Hard
* Early

Sentence:

✅ He runs fast.

---

## Comparative Degree

Uses **-er** or **more**.

Examples:

Fast → Faster

Quickly → More quickly

Sentence:

✅ He runs faster than Rahul.

---

## Superlative Degree

Uses **-est** or **most**.

Examples:

Fast → Fastest

Quickly → Most quickly

Sentence:

✅ He runs the fastest in the team.

---

# 📊 Comparison Table

| Positive  | Comparative    | Superlative    |
| --------- | -------------- | -------------- |
| Fast      | Faster         | Fastest        |
| Hard      | Harder         | Hardest        |
| Early     | Earlier        | Earliest       |
| Quickly   | More Quickly   | Most Quickly   |
| Carefully | More Carefully | Most Carefully |

---

# ⚠️ Irregular Adverbs

Some adverbs do not follow normal comparison rules.

| Positive | Comparative     | Superlative       |
| -------- | --------------- | ----------------- |
| Well     | Better          | Best              |
| Badly    | Worse           | Worst             |
| Little   | Less            | Least             |
| Much     | More            | Most              |
| Far      | Farther/Further | Farthest/Furthest |

---

# 🚫 Common Mistakes

## Mistake 1

❌ He runs quick.

✅ He runs quickly.

---

## Mistake 2

❌ She speaks more faster.

✅ She speaks faster.

---

## Mistake 3

❌ He did good.

✅ He did well.

---

## Mistake 4

❌ I am real happy.

✅ I am really happy.

---

## Mistake 5

❌ She sings beautiful.

✅ She sings beautifully.

---

# 💡 Tips for Placement Exams

* Identify whether the sentence needs an adjective or an adverb.
* Watch out for words ending in **-ly**.
* Remember irregular forms like **well → better → best**.
* Check the position of the adverb in the sentence.
