---

# 4️⃣ Possessive Pronouns

## 📖 Definition

**Possessive Pronouns** show ownership or belonging.

They tell us **who owns something**.

---

# 🌍 Common Possessive Pronouns

| Person | Possessive Pronoun |
| ------ | ------------------ |
| I      | Mine               |
| We     | Ours               |
| You    | Yours              |
| He     | His                |
| She    | Hers               |
| They   | Theirs             |
| It     | Its                |

---

# 📝 Examples

### Example 1

> This book is **mine**.

Mine shows ownership.

---

### Example 2

> That car is **hers**.

Hers shows that the car belongs to her.

---

### Example 3

> This house is **ours**.

Ours shows ownership.

---

# ⚠️ Common Mistake

❌ This book is my.

✅ This book is mine.

Reason:

"My" needs a noun after it.

Example:

✅ This is my book.

---

# 5️⃣ Demonstrative Pronouns

## 📖 Definition

Demonstrative Pronouns point to specific people, things, or ideas.

They tell us **which one** we are talking about.

---

# 🌍 Demonstrative Pronouns

| Near  | Far   |
| ----- | ----- |
| This  | That  |
| These | Those |

---

# 📝 Examples

### Example 1

> **This** is my laptop.

This → Points to a nearby object.

---

### Example 2

> **That** is your house.

That → Points to something far.

---

### Example 3

> **These** are my books.

These → Multiple things nearby.

---

### Example 4

> **Those** are beautiful flowers.

Those → Multiple things far away.

---

# 🧠 Memory Trick

```text id="demo01"
This  → One near

That  → One far

These → Many near

Those → Many far
```

---

# 6️⃣ Reflexive Pronouns

## 📖 Definition

Reflexive Pronouns refer back to the subject of the sentence.

They usually end with:

```text id="reflex01"
-self
-selves
```

---

# 🌍 List of Reflexive Pronouns

| Subject | Reflexive  |
| ------- | ---------- |
| I       | Myself     |
| We      | Ourselves  |
| You     | Yourself   |
| He      | Himself    |
| She     | Herself    |
| It      | Itself     |
| They    | Themselves |

---

# 📝 Examples

### Example 1

> I completed the project myself.

Myself refers back to I.

---

### Example 2

> She prepared herself for the interview.

Herself refers back to she.

---

### Example 3

> They enjoyed themselves.

Themselves refers back to they.

---

# ⚠️ Common Mistake

❌ Myself Gunjan.

✅ I am Gunjan.

"Myself" cannot be used as a subject.

---

# 7️⃣ Relative Pronouns

## 📖 Definition

Relative Pronouns connect a noun with extra information about it.

---

# 🌍 Common Relative Pronouns

* Who
* Whom
* Whose
* Which
* That

---

# 📝 Examples

### Example 1

> The boy **who** won the race is my friend.

Who connects information about the boy.

---

### Example 2

> This is the book **which** I bought.

Which connects book with extra information.

---

### Example 3

> The person **whose** phone was lost is here.

Whose shows possession.

---

# 8️⃣ Interrogative Pronouns

## 📖 Definition

Interrogative Pronouns are used to ask questions.

---

# 🌍 Common Interrogative Pronouns

* Who
* Whom
* Whose
* What
* Which

---

# 📝 Examples

### Example 1

> **Who** is your teacher?

---

### Example 2

> **What** is your name?

---

### Example 3

> **Which** is your bag?

---

# 9️⃣ Indefinite Pronouns

## 📖 Definition

Indefinite Pronouns refer to people or things in a general way without specifying exactly.

---

# 🌍 Examples

* Someone
* Somebody
* Anyone
* Everyone
* Nobody
* Something
* Nothing
* Everything

---

# 📝 Examples

### Example 1

> Everyone likes success.

Everyone → Indefinite Pronoun

---

### Example 2

> Someone called you.

Someone → Indefinite Pronoun

---

### Example 3

> Nobody knows the answer.

Nobody → Indefinite Pronoun

---

# 🔟 Reciprocal Pronouns

## 📖 Definition

Reciprocal Pronouns show a mutual relationship or action between two or more people.

---

# 🌍 Reciprocal Pronouns

* Each other
* One another

---

# 📝 Examples

### Example 1

> They help each other.

---

### Example 2

> The students respect one another.

---

# 📊 Complete Pronoun Summary Table

| Type          | Function                | Examples          |
| ------------- | ----------------------- | ----------------- |
| Personal      | Refers to people/things | I, He, They       |
| Possessive    | Shows ownership         | Mine, Yours       |
| Demonstrative | Points something        | This, Those       |
| Reflexive     | Refers back             | Myself, Herself   |
| Relative      | Joins clauses           | Who, Which        |
| Interrogative | Asks questions          | What, Who         |
| Indefinite    | General reference       | Everyone, Someone |
| Reciprocal    | Mutual action           | Each other        |

```text id="pronountrick02"
Ownership → Possessive

Mutual Action → Reciprocal
```

---

## Error 2

✅ This is her book.

---

## Error 3

❌ Myself will complete this work.

✅ I will complete this work.

❌ Everyone have a dream.

✅ Everyone has a dream.
