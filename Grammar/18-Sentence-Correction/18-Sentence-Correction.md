# 📖 What is Sentence Correction?

**Sentence Correction** is the process of identifying and fixing grammatical, vocabulary, punctuation, or sentence structure mistakes to make a sentence accurate, meaningful, and professional.

It is one of the most important topics in:

- Campus Placements
- SSC
- Banking Exams
- UPSC
- CAT
- IELTS
- Job Interviews
- Professional Communication

# 🧩 Types of Sentence Errors

| Error Type | Example |
|------------|---------|
| Subject–Verb Agreement | He go → He goes |
| Tense | Yesterday I go → Yesterday I went |
| Pronoun | Me is ready → I am ready |
| Article | He is engineer → He is an engineer |
| Preposition | Discuss about → Discuss |
| Word Order | Always I wake up → I always wake up |
| Double Negative | I don't know nothing → I don't know anything |
| Punctuation | Hello , Rahul → Hello, Rahul |

# 🟢 Subject–Verb Agreement Errors

The verb must match the subject in **number** and **person**.

### Incorrect

❌ She write beautifully.

### Correct

✅ She writes beautifully.

---

### Incorrect

❌ They plays football.

### Correct

✅ They play football.

---

### Incorrect

❌ One of the students are absent.

### Correct

✅ One of the students is absent.

---

# 🔵 Tense Errors

Use the correct tense according to the time of action.

### Incorrect

❌ Yesterday I go to school.

### Correct

✅ Yesterday I went to school.

---

### Incorrect

❌ She has completed the assignment yesterday.

### Correct

✅ She completed the assignment yesterday.

---

### Incorrect

❌ I am knowing the answer.

### Correct

✅ I know the answer.

---

# 🟣 Pronoun Errors

Pronouns should correctly refer to the subject.

### Incorrect

❌ Me and Rahul completed the project.

### Correct

✅ Rahul and I completed the project.

---

### Incorrect

❌ Him is my cousin.

### Correct

✅ He is my cousin.

---

### Incorrect

❌ Everyone should submit their form. *(Formal Grammar)*

### Correct

✅ Everyone should submit his or her form.

> **Note:** Modern English commonly accepts **their** as a singular, gender-neutral pronoun.
