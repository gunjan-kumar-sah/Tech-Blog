# 4️⃣ Linking Verb

## 📖 Definition

A **Linking Verb** connects the subject with information that describes or identifies it.

It does not show an action. It shows a **state or condition**.

---

# 🌍 Common Linking Verbs

### Forms of "Be"

* am
* is
* are
* was
* were
* been

### Other Linking Verbs

* become
* seem
* appear
* look
* feel
* sound
* taste
* smell
* remain

---

# 📝 Examples

### Example 1

> She **is** a doctor.

"Is" connects She with doctor.

---

### Example 2

> The food **tastes** delicious.

"Tastes" describes the food.

---

### Example 3

> He **became** successful.

"Became" shows a change of state.

---

# 💡 Memory Trick

```text id="link01"
Action Verb → Does something

Linking Verb → Describes something
```

Example:

Action:

> He runs fast.

Linking:

> He is happy.

---

# 5️⃣ Transitive Verb

## 📖 Definition

A **Transitive Verb** is a verb that needs an object to complete its meaning.

Formula:

```text id="trans01"
Subject + Verb + Object
```

---

# 🌍 Examples

### Example 1

> She writes a letter.

She → Subject

Writes → Verb

Letter → Object

---

### Example 2

> I bought a laptop.

Bought → Verb

Laptop → Object

---

### Example 3

> Rahul plays cricket.

Plays → Verb

Cricket → Object

---

# 🔍 How to Identify Transitive Verb?

Ask:

> Verb + What?

or

> Verb + Whom?

Example:

She reads.

Reads what?

Answer:

A book.

Therefore, reads is transitive.

---

# 6️⃣ Intransitive Verb

## 📖 Definition

An **Intransitive Verb** does not need an object to complete its meaning.

Formula:

```text id="intrans01"
Subject + Verb
```

---

# 🌍 Examples

### Example 1

> The baby cried.

Cried does not need an object.

---

### Example 2

> Birds fly.

Fly is complete.

---

### Example 3

> He sleeps.

Sleeps does not require an object.

---

# ⚖️ Transitive vs Intransitive Verb

| Transitive Verb     | Intransitive Verb         |
| ------------------- | ------------------------- |
| Needs object        | Does not need object      |
| Action transfers    | Action stays with subject |
| She writes a letter | She writes                |

---

# 7️⃣ Regular Verb

## 📖 Definition

A **Regular Verb** forms its past tense by adding:

```text id="regular01"
-ed
```

---

# 🌍 Examples

| V1    | V2      | V3      |
| ----- | ------- | ------- |
| Play  | Played  | Played  |
| Walk  | Walked  | Walked  |
| Work  | Worked  | Worked  |
| Clean | Cleaned | Cleaned |

---

# 📝 Sentences

Present:

> I play cricket.

Past:

> I played cricket.

---

Present:

> She works hard.

Past:

> She worked hard.

---

# 8️⃣ Irregular Verb

## 📖 Definition

An **Irregular Verb** does not follow the normal "-ed" rule.

Their past forms change differently.

---

# 🌍 Examples

| V1    | V2    | V3      |
| ----- | ----- | ------- |
| Go    | Went  | Gone    |
| Eat   | Ate   | Eaten   |
| Write | Wrote | Written |
| See   | Saw   | Seen    |
| Come  | Came  | Come    |
| Take  | Took  | Taken   |

---

# 📝 Sentences

Present:

> I go to school.

Past:

> I went to school.

---

Present:

> She writes a story.

Past:

> She wrote a story.

---

# 📚 Verb Forms (V1, V2, V3)

Verb forms are important for understanding tenses.

| Form | Meaning         | Example |
| ---- | --------------- | ------- |
| V1   | Base Form       | Go      |
| V2   | Past Form       | Went    |
| V3   | Past Participle | Gone    |

---

# 🌍 Common Verb Forms

| V1    | V2       | V3     |
| ----- | -------- | ------ |
| Be    | Was/Were | Been   |
| Do    | Did      | Done   |
| Make  | Made     | Made   |
| Have  | Had      | Had    |
| Give  | Gave     | Given  |
| Speak | Spoke    | Spoken |
| Break | Broke    | Broken |

---

# 🧠 Verb Identification Tricks

## Trick 1

Words showing action:

```text id="actiontrick"
Run
Write
Play
Study
Eat
```

are usually verbs.

---

## Trick 2

Words showing condition:

```text id="state01"
am
is
are
was
were
```

are verbs.

---

## Trick 3

If a word changes with time:

Example:

```text id="timechange"
Go → Went → Gone
```

It is a verb.

# ⚠️ Common Verb Mistakes

## Mistake 1

❌ He go to school.

✅ He goes to school.

---

## Mistake 2

❌ She don't like coffee.

✅ She doesn't like coffee.

---

## Mistake 3

❌ I have went there.

✅ I have gone there.

---

## Mistake 4

❌ He can sings.

✅ He can sing.
