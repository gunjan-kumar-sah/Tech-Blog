# 📝 Verb Practice Questions

Identify the type of verb or choose the correct option.

---

## Question 1

Rahul **plays** football.

The word "plays" is:

A. Noun
B. Verb
C. Adjective
D. Pronoun

✅ **Answer:** B. Verb

---

## Question 2

She **is reading** a book.

"Is" is a:

A. Main Verb
B. Helping Verb
C. Noun
D. Adverb

✅ **Answer:** B. Helping Verb

---

## Question 3

They **completed** the project.

"Completed" is a:

A. Main Verb
B. Pronoun
C. Adjective
D. Preposition

✅ **Answer:** A. Main Verb

---

## Question 4

The baby cried.

"Cried" is:

A. Transitive Verb
B. Intransitive Verb
C. Helping Verb
D. Linking Verb

✅ **Answer:** B. Intransitive Verb

---

## Question 5

She writes a letter.

"Writes" is:

A. Transitive Verb
B. Intransitive Verb
C. Noun
D. Adjective

✅ **Answer:** A. Transitive Verb

# 🚀 Professional English Tip

Strong verbs make communication powerful.

Weak:

❌ I did work on the project.

Better:

✅ I developed the project.

Weak:

❌ I made a website.

Better:

✅ I created a responsive website.

# ⚠️ Common Verb Errors

## Error 1: Wrong Verb Form

❌ He has went home.

✅ He has gone home.

---

## Error 2: Modal Verb Mistake

❌ She can sings.

✅ She can sing.

Rule:

After modal verbs, always use V1.

---

## Error 3: Subject-Verb Agreement

❌ They plays cricket.

✅ They play cricket.

---

## Error 4: Double Past Usage

❌ I did not went there.

✅ I did not go there.

# 📄 Verb Cheat Sheet

```text id="verb-final"
VERB

Definition:
A word that shows action, state, or occurrence.

Types:

1. Main Verb
→ Main action

2. Helping Verb
→ Supports main verb

3. Linking Verb
→ Shows state

4. Action Verb
→ Shows action

5. Transitive Verb
→ Needs object

6. Intransitive Verb
→ No object

7. Regular Verb
→ Past ends with -ed

8. Irregular Verb
→ Changes form


Important Forms:

V1 → Base Form
V2 → Past Form
V3 → Past Participle
```
