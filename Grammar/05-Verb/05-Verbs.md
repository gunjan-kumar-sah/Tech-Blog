# 📖 What is a Verb?

A **Verb** is a word that shows:

* An action
* A state
* An occurrence

In simple words:

> **A verb tells us what someone or something does or what happens.**

---

# 🌍 Examples of Verbs

| Verb   | Meaning       |
| ------ | ------------- |
| Run    | Action        |
| Eat    | Action        |
| Write  | Action        |
| Study  | Action        |
| Think  | Mental action |
| Is     | State         |
| Are    | State         |
| Become | Change        |

---

---

### Example 2

> She **writes** a blog.

Writes → Verb

---

### Example 3

> They **are** happy.

Are → Verb

---

### Example 4

> I **learn** programming.

Learn → Verb

# 🎯 Why Are Verbs Important?

Without verbs, sentences cannot express complete meaning.

Example:

❌ Rahul cricket.

This sentence is incomplete.

Adding verb:

✅ Rahul plays cricket.

Now the sentence has meaning.

---

# 🔍 How to Identify a Verb?

Ask:

### Question 1:

**What is the subject doing?**

Example:

> The boy runs fast.

What is the boy doing?

Answer:

Running → Verb

---

### Question 2:

**What is happening?**

Example:

> She is reading.

What is happening?

Answer:

Reading → Verb

---

### Question 3:

**What is the condition/state?**

Example:

> He is tired.

State:

Is → Verb

---

# 🧩 Types of Verbs

English verbs are mainly divided into:

| No. | Type              | Meaning              |
| :-: | ----------------- | -------------------- |
|  1  | Main Verb         | Main action/state    |
|  2  | Helping Verb      | Supports main verb   |
|  3  | Action Verb       | Shows action         |
|  4  | Linking Verb      | Connects subject     |
|  5  | Transitive Verb   | Needs object         |
|  6  | Intransitive Verb | Does not need object |
|  7  | Regular Verb      | Normal past form     |
|  8  | Irregular Verb    | Different past form  |

---

# 1️⃣ Main Verb

## 📖 Definition

A **Main Verb** is the most important verb in a sentence.

It shows the actual action or state.

---

# 🌍 Examples

| Sentence            | Main Verb |
| ------------------- | --------- |
| I play football.    | Play      |
| She sings well.     | Sings     |
| They study English. | Study     |
| He works hard.      | Works     |

---

# 📝 More Examples

✅ I **write** code.

Write → Main Verb

---

✅ She **teaches** English.

Teaches → Main Verb

---

# 2️⃣ Helping Verb

## 📖 Definition

A **Helping Verb** supports the main verb to show:

* Tense
* Time
* Possibility
* Condition

---

# 🌍 Common Helping Verbs

### Forms of Be:

* am
* is
* are
* was
* were
* been
* being

---

### Forms of Have:

* have
* has
* had

---

### Forms of Do:

* do
* does
* did

---

### Modal Helping Verbs:

* can
* could
* may
* might
* will
* would
* should
* must

---

# 📝 Examples

### Example 1

> She **is** reading a book.

Helping Verb:

is

Main Verb:

reading

---

### Example 2

> They **have** completed the project.

Helping Verb:

have

Main Verb:

completed

---

### Example 3

> I **can** solve this problem.

Helping Verb:

can

Main Verb:

solve

---

# 🧠 Memory Trick

Remember:

```text id="verb01"
Main Verb = Main Action

Helping Verb = Helps Main Verb
```

Example:

```text id="verb02"
She is studying.

is → Helping Verb

studying → Main Verb
```

# 3️⃣ Action Verb

## 📖 Definition

Action Verbs show physical or mental actions.

---

# 🌍 Examples

Physical Actions:

* Run
* Jump
* Eat
* Write
* Play

Mental Actions:

* Think
* Know
* Believe
* Understand

---

# 📝 Sentences

✅ He runs every morning.

Runs → Action Verb

---

✅ I understand the concept.

Understand → Action Verb
