# 🔵 Inference Questions

## 📖 What is an Inference?

An **inference** is a logical conclusion based on the information provided in the passage. The answer is **not stated directly**, but it can be understood from the given facts.

---

## Example Passage

Rahul carried an umbrella and wore a raincoat before leaving home.

### Question

What can be inferred?

A. It was probably raining.

B. Rahul was going to school.

C. Rahul bought a new umbrella.

D. Rahul dislikes sunny weather.

✅ **Answer:** **A. It was probably raining.**

---

# 🟢 Fact vs Opinion

Understanding the difference between facts and opinions is essential.

---

## Fact

A **fact** can be verified.

### Example

The Earth revolves around the Sun.

✅ This is a fact.

---

## Opinion

An **opinion** expresses a personal belief or judgment.

### Example

Python is the best programming language.

✅ This is an opinion.

---

# 🟣 Author's Tone

The **tone** shows the author's attitude toward the topic.

---

## Common Tones

| Tone | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| Informative | Giving information |
| Persuasive | Convincing the reader |
| Critical | Pointing out faults |
| Humorous | Funny or entertaining |
| Optimistic | Positive and hopeful |
| Pessimistic | Negative or doubtful |
| Neutral | No strong emotion |
| Appreciative | Showing admiration |

---

## Example

"Artificial Intelligence is transforming industries and creating new opportunities."

Tone:

✅ **Optimistic**

---

# 🟡 Author's Purpose

Why did the author write the passage?

---

## Main Purposes

- Inform
- Explain
- Persuade
- Entertain
- Describe
- Compare
- Argue

---

## Example

"The benefits of regular exercise include better health and reduced stress."

Purpose:

✅ **To Inform**

---

# 🔴 Main Idea Questions

The **main idea** is the central message of the passage.

---

## Example

Passage:

Technology has changed education by making learning more flexible, interactive, and accessible.

Question:

What is the main idea?

✅ Technology has improved education.

---

# ⚪ Supporting Details

Supporting details explain or strengthen the main idea.

---

## Example

Main Idea:

Exercise improves health.

Supporting Details:

- Reduces stress
- Improves heart health
- Builds stamina
- Increases energy

# 📚 Advanced Reading Tips

✔ Read actively.

✔ Focus on keywords.

✔ Ignore unnecessary details.

✔ Don't memorize the passage.

✔ Understand paragraph connections.

✔ Eliminate incorrect options.

✔ Avoid assumptions.

✔ Use evidence from the passage.

---

# ⚠️ Common Errors

### Error 1

Reading every word slowly.

✅ Read strategically.

---

### Error 2

Making assumptions not supported by the passage.

✅ Use only given information.

---

### Error 3

Ignoring transition words.

Examples:

- However
- Therefore
- Although
- Because
- Consequently

---

### Error 4

Choosing an answer based on personal opinion.

✅ Choose the answer supported by the passage.

---

### Error 5

Ignoring the author's tone.

Always identify whether the author is informing, persuading, criticizing, or entertaining.
