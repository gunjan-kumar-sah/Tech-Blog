# 📖 What is Reading Comprehension?

**Reading Comprehension (RC)** is the ability to **read, understand, analyze, and interpret** a written passage correctly.

Instead of simply reading words, you learn to understand:

- The main idea
- Supporting details
- Vocabulary
- Author's purpose
- Tone
- Inference
- Logical conclusions

# 📚 Types of Reading Comprehension

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Factual | Based on facts directly given in the passage |
| Inferential | Requires logical thinking beyond the text |
| Vocabulary-Based | Meaning of words from context |
| Analytical | Understand arguments and opinions |
| Critical | Evaluate the author's viewpoint |

# 🟢 Skimming

## Definition

**Skimming** means reading quickly to understand the **main idea**.

### Focus On

- Title
- Headings
- First paragraph
- Last paragraph
- Topic sentences

---

### Example

Passage Title:

**Artificial Intelligence in Education**

After skimming, you understand:

"The passage explains how AI improves education."

---

# 🔵 Scanning

## Definition

**Scanning** means searching for **specific information**.

---

### Examples

Find:

- Date
- Name
- Number
- Place
- Keyword

---

### Example

Question:

"When was Python created?"

Instead of reading everything, scan the passage for the year.

---

# 🟣 Vocabulary in Context

Sometimes a word has different meanings depending on the sentence.

Example:

"The company launched a new product."

The word **launched** means:

✅ Introduced

NOT

❌ Threw

---

Another Example

"The bright student solved the problem."

Bright means:

✅ Intelligent

NOT

❌ Shiny

# 🌍 Basic Reading Example

## Passage

Rahul loves reading books. Every evening he spends one hour reading technology and science magazines because he wants to become a software engineer.

---

## Question 1

What does Rahul enjoy?

✅ Reading books.

---

## Question 2

Why does Rahul read technology magazines?

✅ Because he wants to become a software engineer.

---

## Question 3

How much time does Rahul spend reading?

✅ One hour every evening.

# 📋 Basic Reading Tips

### Tip 1

Read the title first.

---

### Tip 2

Identify the main idea.

---

### Tip 3

Underline important keywords.

---

### Tip 4

Read the questions carefully.

---

### Tip 5

Never assume information that is not given.

---

### Tip 6

Answer only using evidence from the passage.
