# 📝 Practice Questions

Test your understanding of **Punctuation**.

---

## Question 1

Choose the correct sentence.

A. hello, everyone.

B. Hello everyone

C. Hello, everyone.

D. Hello, everyone

✅ **Answer:** **C. Hello, everyone.**

---

## Question 2

Which punctuation mark ends a question?

A. .

B. ,

C. ?

D. !

✅ **Answer:** **C. ?**

---

## Question 3

Choose the correct sentence.

A. Wow!!!!

B. Wow!

C. Wow..

D. Wow,

✅ **Answer:** **B. Wow!**

---

## Question 4

Which punctuation mark is used to introduce a list?

A. ;

B. :

C. ,

D. -

✅ **Answer:** **B. :**

---

## Question 5

Choose the correct sentence.

A. Rahuls book

B. Rahul's book

C. Rahul,s book

D. Rahul"s book

✅ **Answer:** **B. Rahul's book**

---

## Question 6

Which punctuation mark joins two closely related independent clauses?

A. ,

B. :

C. ;

D. !

✅ **Answer:** **C. ;**

---

## Question 7

Choose the correct sentence.

A. She said I am ready.

B. She said, "I am ready."

C. She said: I am ready.

D. She said; "I am ready."

✅ **Answer:** **B. She said, "I am ready."**

---

## Question 8

Which punctuation mark shows omitted words or a pause?

A. —

B. -

C. ...

D. '

✅ **Answer:** **C. ...**

---

## Question 9

Choose the correct compound adjective.

A. High quality product

B. High-quality product

C. High, quality product

D. High; quality product

✅ **Answer:** **B. High-quality product**

---

## Question 10

Which sentence is punctuated correctly?

A. I bought apples bananas oranges.

B. I bought apples, bananas, oranges.

C. I bought apples; bananas; oranges.

D. I bought apples: bananas: oranges.

✅ **Answer:** **B. I bought apples, bananas, oranges.**

# 🎯 Placement Level MCQs

### Q1. Which sentence is grammatically correct?

A. The meeting starts at 9:00 AM.

B. The meeting starts at 9;00 AM.

C. The meeting starts at 9,00 AM.

D. The meeting starts at 9.00 AM.

✅ **Answer:** **A**

---

### Q2. Which punctuation mark is used for possession?

A. :

B. '

C. ;

D. -

✅ **Answer:** **B**

---

### Q3. Which sentence correctly uses a semicolon?

A. She likes reading, he likes writing.

B. She likes reading; he likes writing.

C. She likes reading: he likes writing.

D. She likes reading - he likes writing.

✅ **Answer:** **B**

---

### Q4. Which sentence correctly uses parentheses?

A. Python created by Guido van Rossum is popular.

B. Python (created by Guido van Rossum) is popular.

C. Python: created by Guido van Rossum is popular.

D. Python-created by Guido van Rossum-is popular.

✅ **Answer:** **B**

---

### Q5. Which punctuation mark is most appropriate for strong emotion?

A. ,

B. .

C. !

D. ;

✅ **Answer:** **C**

# 💼 Punctuation in Professional Writing

Correct punctuation improves clarity and professionalism in resumes, emails, reports, and documentation.

---

## Resume Example

❌ Skilled in Python Java SQL HTML CSS

✅ Skilled in **Python, Java, SQL, HTML, and CSS.**

---

## Email Example

❌ Dear Sir

I hope you are doing well

Regards

Gunjan

✅ Dear Sir,

I hope you are doing well.

Regards,
Gunjan Kumar Sah

---

## Report Example

❌ Project completed team tested application deployed

✅ The project was completed, the team tested the application, and it was successfully deployed.

---

## LinkedIn Example

❌ Excited to share I completed my internship

✅ Excited to share that I completed my internship! Thank you to everyone who supported me.

# ⚠️ Common Errors

### Error 1

❌ Hello , everyone.

✅ Hello, everyone.

---

### Error 2

❌ Whats your name?

✅ What's your name?

---

### Error 3

❌ I completed my work, now I can relax.

✅ I completed my work; now I can relax.

---

### Error 4

❌ The meeting starts at 9;30 AM.

✅ The meeting starts at 9:30 AM.

---

### Error 5

❌ She said I am ready.

✅ She said, "I am ready."

# 📄 Punctuation Cheat Sheet

```text id="punctuationfinal"

FULL STOP (.)
→ End of a statement

COMMA (,)
→ Short pause
→ Separate list items

QUESTION MARK (?)
→ End of a question

EXCLAMATION MARK (!)
→ Strong emotion

COLON (:)
→ Introduce a list or explanation

SEMICOLON (;)
→ Join related independent clauses

QUOTATION MARKS (" ")
→ Direct speech

APOSTROPHE (')
→ Possession
→ Contractions

HYPHEN (-)
→ Compound words

DASH (—)
→ Emphasis / interruption

PARENTHESES ( )
→ Extra information

ELLIPSIS (...)
→ Pause / omitted words

```
