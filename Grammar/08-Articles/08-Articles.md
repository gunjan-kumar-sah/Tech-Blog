# 📖 What are Articles?

Articles are small words used before nouns to show whether we are talking about something **specific** or **general**.

English has three articles:

```text id="article01"

A
An
The

```

---

# 🌍 Examples

### Example 1

> I saw **a** dog.

Meaning:

Any dog (not specific)

---

### Example 2

> I saw **the** dog.

Meaning:

A specific dog.

---

### Example 3

> She is **an** engineer.

Meaning:

One person from a group.

---

# 🧩 Types of Articles

Articles are divided into two categories:

| Type                | Articles |
| ------------------- | -------- |
| Indefinite Articles | A, An    |
| Definite Article    | The      |

---

# 1️⃣ Indefinite Articles (A & An)

## 📖 Definition

"A" and "An" are called indefinite articles because they talk about something that is not specific.

---

# Example:

> I bought **a** book.

Meaning:

Any book.

---

> She is **an** artist.

Meaning:

One artist.

---

# 2️⃣ Definite Article (The)

## 📖 Definition

"The" is called a definite article because it refers to a specific person, place, thing, or idea.

---

# Examples:

> The sun rises in the east.

(The sun is unique)

---

> I read the book you gave me.

(A specific book)

---

# 🔥 Difference Between A, An and The

| Article | Usage                  | Example  |
| ------- | ---------------------- | -------- |
| A       | Before consonant sound | a book   |
| An      | Before vowel sound     | an apple |
| The     | Specific things        | the moon |

---

# 1️⃣ Rules of "A"

## Rule 1:

Use **A** before words starting with consonant sounds.

---

Examples:

✅ a boy

✅ a car

✅ a university

---

# Important:

Article depends on **sound**, not spelling.

---

# Examples:

### University

Word starts with "U"

But pronunciation:

"yu-niversity"

Sound = consonant

Correct:

✅ a university

---

### European

Pronunciation:

"yu-ropean"

Correct:

✅ a European

---

# Rule 2:

Use "A" before singular countable nouns.

---

Examples:

✅ a pen

✅ a laptop

✅ a student

---

Incorrect:

❌ a water

❌ a milk

---

# Rule 3:

Use "A" when introducing something for the first time.

---

Example:

> I saw a man in the park.

(First time mention)

---

Later:

> The man was wearing a blue shirt.

(Specific man)

---

# 2️⃣ Rules of "An"

## Rule 1:

Use **An** before vowel sounds.

Vowel sounds:

* A
* E
* I
* O
* U

---

Examples:

✅ an apple

✅ an elephant

✅ an idea

✅ an orange

---

# Rule 2:

Use "An" before silent "h".

---

Examples:

✅ an hour

✅ an honest person

✅ an heir

---

Reason:

h is not pronounced.

---

# Rule 3:

Use "An" before abbreviations with vowel sounds.

---

Examples:

✅ an MBA

(M = "em")

---

✅ an FIR

(F = "ef")

---

# ⚠️ Common Mistakes

## Mistake 1

❌ He is a honest man.

✅ He is an honest man.

---

## Mistake 2

❌ She is an university student.

✅ She is a university student.

---

## Mistake 3

❌ I need a water.

✅ I need water.

---

# 🧠 Memory Trick

```text id="articletrick"

Sound decides the article.

Vowel Sound
      ↓
     AN


Consonant Sound
      ↓
      A


Specific Thing
      ↓
     THE

```

---

# 📌 Quick Revision

```text id="articlequick"

Articles:

A
→ General + Consonant Sound


An
→ General + Vowel Sound


The
→ Specific / Unique Things


Examples:

a book

an apple

the sun

```
