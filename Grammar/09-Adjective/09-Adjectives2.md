# 6️⃣ Interrogative Adjectives

## 📖 Definition

Interrogative adjectives are used with nouns to ask questions.

Common interrogative adjectives:

* Which
* What
* Whose

---

# Examples

### Which

✅ Which book do you want?

(Book = noun)

---

### What

✅ What subject are you studying?

(Subject = noun)

---

### Whose

✅ Whose laptop is this?

(Laptop = noun)

---

# 7️⃣ Proper Adjectives

## 📖 Definition

Proper adjectives are formed from proper nouns.

They describe nationality, place, culture, or origin.

---

# Examples

| Proper Noun | Proper Adjective |
| ----------- | ---------------- |
| India       | Indian           |
| America     | American         |
| Japan       | Japanese         |
| France      | French           |

---

# Sentences

✅ Indian food is famous worldwide.

✅ Japanese technology is advanced.

✅ American companies are popular.

---

# 8️⃣ Distributive Adjectives

## 📖 Definition

Distributive adjectives refer to individual members of a group.

Common words:

* Each
* Every
* Either
* Neither

---

# Examples

### Each

✅ Each student has a notebook.

---

### Every

✅ Every employee follows the rules.

---

### Either

✅ Either option is correct.

---

### Neither

✅ Neither answer is correct.

# 🔥 Degrees of Comparison

## 📖 Definition

Degrees of comparison show comparison between people, places, or things.

There are three degrees:

1. Positive Degree
2. Comparative Degree
3. Superlative Degree

---

# 1️⃣ Positive Degree

## 📖 Definition

Positive degree describes one person or thing without comparison.

---

# Formula

```text id="positive01"
Subject + is/am/are + Adjective
```

---

# Examples

✅ Rahul is tall.

✅ This book is interesting.

✅ She is intelligent.

---

# 2️⃣ Comparative Degree

## 📖 Definition

Comparative degree compares two people or things.

Usually uses:

* than

---

# Formula

```text id="comparative01"
Subject + is + Adjective + er + than
```

or

```text id="comparative02"
Subject + is + more + Adjective + than
```

---

# Examples

Positive:

> Rahul is tall.

Comparative:

> Rahul is taller than Amit.

---

Positive:

> This book is interesting.

Comparative:

> This book is more interesting than that book.

---

# 3️⃣ Superlative Degree

## 📖 Definition

Superlative degree compares more than two people or things.

It shows the highest degree.

Uses:

* the
* est
* most

---

# Formula

```text id="super01"
Subject + is + the + Adjective + est
```

or

```text id="super02"
Subject + is + the most + Adjective
```

---

# Examples

Positive:

> Rahul is tall.

Comparative:

> Rahul is taller than Amit.

Superlative:

> Rahul is the tallest student in the class.

---

# 📊 Degree Comparison Examples

| Positive    | Comparative      | Superlative      |
| ----------- | ---------------- | ---------------- |
| Tall        | Taller           | Tallest          |
| Small       | Smaller          | Smallest         |
| Fast        | Faster           | Fastest          |
| Big         | Bigger           | Biggest          |
| Beautiful   | More beautiful   | Most beautiful   |
| Intelligent | More intelligent | Most intelligent |

---

# 🧠 Rules for Making Comparative & Superlative

---

# Rule 1: One-Syllable Adjectives

Add:

Comparative → **er**

Superlative → **est**

---

Examples:

| Positive | Comparative | Superlative |
| -------- | ----------- | ----------- |
| Tall     | Taller      | Tallest     |
| Short    | Shorter     | Shortest    |
| Fast     | Faster      | Fastest     |

---

# Rule 2: Ending with "e"

Add:

Comparative → r

Superlative → st

---

Examples:

| Positive | Comparative | Superlative |
| -------- | ----------- | ----------- |
| Large    | Larger      | Largest     |
| Nice     | Nicer       | Nicest      |

---

# Rule 3: Short Vowel + Consonant

Double the last consonant.

---

Examples:

Big:

Comparative:

✅ Bigger

Superlative:

✅ Biggest

---

Hot:

✅ Hotter

✅ Hottest

---

# Rule 4: Two or More Syllable Adjectives

Use:

more / most

---

Examples:

Beautiful:

✅ More beautiful

✅ Most beautiful

---

Expensive:

✅ More expensive

✅ Most expensive

---

# Rule 5: Irregular Adjectives

Some adjectives don't follow normal rules.

---

| Positive | Comparative | Superlative |
| -------- | ----------- | ----------- |
| Good     | Better      | Best        |
| Bad      | Worse       | Worst       |
| Little   | Less        | Least       |
| Much     | More        | Most        |
| Many     | More        | Most        |
| Far      | Farther     | Farthest    |

---

# ⚠️ Common Mistakes

## Mistake 1

❌ He is more taller than me.

✅ He is taller than me.

---

## Mistake 2

❌ She is most beautiful girl.

✅ She is the most beautiful girl.

---

## Mistake 3

❌ This is the bestest book.

✅ This is the best book.
