# 📚 Pronoun Change Rules

When converting **Direct Speech** to **Indirect Speech**, pronouns usually change according to the speaker, listener, and context.

---

## 📝 First Person Pronouns

First person pronouns (**I, me, my, we, our, us**) change according to the **subject of the reporting verb**.

### Example

**Direct:**

Rahul said, "I am tired."

**Indirect:**

Rahul said that **he** was tired.

---

**Direct:**

She said, "My laptop is new."

**Indirect:**

She said that **her** laptop was new.

---

# 📝 Second Person Pronouns

Second person pronouns (**you, your, yours**) change according to the **object/listener**.

### Example

**Direct:**

The teacher said to me, "You are hardworking."

**Indirect:**

The teacher told me that **I** was hardworking.

---

# 📝 Third Person Pronouns

Third person pronouns (**he, she, they, it**) usually remain unchanged.

### Example

**Direct:**

Rahul said, "He is my friend."

**Indirect:**

Rahul said that **he** was his friend.

---

# 📅 Time & Place Word Changes

Certain words change in indirect speech when the reporting verb is in the past tense.

| Direct Speech | Indirect Speech                   |
| ------------- | --------------------------------- |
| Now           | Then                              |
| Today         | That day                          |
| Tomorrow      | The next day / The following day  |
| Yesterday     | The previous day / The day before |
| Tonight       | That night                        |
| Last week     | The previous week                 |
| Next month    | The following month               |
| Here          | There                             |
| This          | That                              |
| These         | Those                             |
| Ago           | Before                            |

---

## Examples

### Direct

She said, "I will come **tomorrow**."

### Indirect

She said that she would come **the next day**.

---

### Direct

Rahul said, "I live **here**."

### Indirect

Rahul said that he lived **there**.

---

# ❓ Interrogative Sentences

Questions follow different rules in indirect speech.

---

## Rule 1: Yes/No Questions

Use **if** or **whether**.

### Direct

He said, "Do you like coffee?"

### Indirect

He asked **if** I liked coffee.

---

### Direct

She said, "Are you ready?"

### Indirect

She asked **whether** I was ready.

---

## Rule 2: WH-Questions

Keep the **question word** (what, where, when, why, who, how).

### Direct

He said, "Where do you live?"

### Indirect

He asked where I lived.

---

### Direct

She said, "Why are you late?"

### Indirect

She asked why I was late.

---

# 📢 Imperative Sentences

Imperative sentences express:

* Commands
* Requests
* Advice
* Suggestions

Use reporting verbs such as:

* ordered
* requested
* advised
* suggested
* instructed

---

## Command

### Direct

The officer said, "Stand up."

### Indirect

The officer **ordered** them to stand up.

---

## Request

### Direct

She said, "Please help me."

### Indirect

She **requested** me to help her.

---

## Advice

### Direct

The teacher said, "Study regularly."

### Indirect

The teacher **advised** the students to study regularly.

---

# 🎉 Exclamatory Sentences

Exclamatory sentences express emotions such as joy, surprise, sorrow, or excitement.

Replace the reporting verb with:

* exclaimed
* exclaimed with joy
* exclaimed with sorrow
* exclaimed with surprise

---

## Joy

### Direct

He said, "Hurray! We won."

### Indirect

He exclaimed with joy that they had won.

---

## Surprise

### Direct

She said, "Wow! This is beautiful."

### Indirect

She exclaimed with surprise that it was beautiful.

---

## Sorrow

### Direct

He said, "Alas! My friend is dead."

### Indirect

He exclaimed with sorrow that his friend was dead.

---

# 📋 Reporting Verb Guide

| Sentence Type | Reporting Verb |
| ------------- | -------------- |
| Statement     | said / told    |
| Question      | asked          |
| Command       | ordered        |
| Request       | requested      |
| Advice        | advised        |
| Suggestion    | suggested      |
| Exclamation   | exclaimed      |

# ⚠️ Common Errors

### Error 1

❌ He asked that I was ready.

✅ He asked if I was ready.

---

### Error 2

❌ She told that she was happy.

✅ She said that she was happy.

OR

✅ She told me that she was happy.

---

### Error 3

❌ The teacher said to study hard.

✅ The teacher advised the students to study hard.

---

### Error 4

❌ He said, "Will you help me?" → He asked that I would help him.

✅ He asked if I would help him.

---

### Error 5

❌ She exclaimed that wow it was beautiful.

✅ She exclaimed with surprise that it was beautiful.
