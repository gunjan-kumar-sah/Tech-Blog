# 📖 What is Direct Speech?

**Direct Speech** repeats the **exact words** spoken by a person.

The spoken words are written inside **quotation marks (" ")**.

---

## 📝 Structure

```text
Reporting Clause + "Reported Speech"
```

---

## 🌍 Examples

✅ Rahul said, **"I am happy."**

✅ She said, **"I like coffee."**

✅ The teacher said, **"Study regularly."**

---

# 📖 What is Indirect Speech?

**Indirect Speech** reports the meaning of what someone said **without using the exact words**.

Quotation marks are removed, and the sentence structure changes.

---

## 📝 Structure

```text
Reporting Clause + that + Reported Clause
```

---

## 🌍 Examples

### Direct

Rahul said, **"I am happy."**

### Indirect

Rahul said **that** he was happy.

---

### Direct

She said, **"I like coffee."**

### Indirect

She said that she liked coffee.

---

# 🧩 Parts of a Reported Sentence

Every reported sentence has two parts.

### 1️⃣ Reporting Clause

The part that introduces the speaker.

Example:

> Rahul **said**

---

### 2️⃣ Reported Clause

The actual words spoken.

Example:

> "I am ready."

---

## Complete Example

**Rahul said, "I am ready."**

* Reporting Clause → Rahul said
* Reported Clause → "I am ready."

---

# 📚 Reporting Verbs

A **Reporting Verb** tells us how something was said.

### Common Reporting Verbs

* Said
* Told
* Asked
* Replied
* Requested
* Ordered
* Advised
* Suggested
* Explained
* Promised

---

## Examples

✅ She **said**, "I am busy."

✅ The teacher **advised**, "Work hard."

✅ He **asked**, "Where are you going?"

---

# 🔄 Basic Rules for Conversion

When changing Direct Speech into Indirect Speech:

### Rule 1

Remove quotation marks.

---

### Rule 2

Use **that** in most statements.

---

### Rule 3

Change the tense (when required).

---

### Rule 4

Change pronouns according to the speaker and listener.

---

### Rule 5

Change time and place words where necessary.

---

# 📚 Tense Change Rules

If the **Reporting Verb** is in the **Past Tense**, the tense usually changes.

---

## Rule 1

### Direct

Present Simple

↓

### Indirect

Past Simple

---

### Example

Direct:

She said, **"I play cricket."**

Indirect:

She said that she **played** cricket.

---

## Rule 2

### Direct

Present Continuous

↓

### Indirect

Past Continuous

---

### Example

Direct:

He said, **"I am reading."**

Indirect:

He said that he **was reading**.

---

## Rule 3

### Direct

Present Perfect

↓

### Indirect

Past Perfect

---

### Example

Direct:

She said, **"I have finished my work."**

Indirect:

She said that she **had finished** her work.

---

## Rule 4

### Direct

Past Simple

↓

### Indirect

Past Perfect

---

### Example

Direct:

Rahul said, **"I completed the project."**

Indirect:

Rahul said that he **had completed** the project.

---

# ⚠️ Common Mistakes

### Mistake 1

❌ She said that I am busy.

✅ She said that she was busy.

---

### Mistake 2

❌ He said that he is reading.

✅ He said that he was reading.

---

### Mistake 3

❌ Rahul said he completed the task.

✅ Rahul said that he had completed the task.
