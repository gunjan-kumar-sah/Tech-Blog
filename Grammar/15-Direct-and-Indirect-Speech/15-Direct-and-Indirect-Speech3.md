# 📝 Practice Questions

Test your understanding of **Direct & Indirect Speech (Narration)**.

---

## Question 1

Choose the correct indirect speech.

**Direct:** Rahul said, *"I am happy."*

A. Rahul said that I was happy.

B. Rahul said that he was happy.

C. Rahul said he is happy.

D. Rahul said that he has been happy.

✅ **Answer:** **B. Rahul said that he was happy.**

---

## Question 2

Choose the correct indirect speech.

**Direct:** She said, *"I have finished my work."*

A. She said that she finished her work.

B. She said that she had finished her work.

C. She said that she has finished her work.

D. She said that she will finish her work.

✅ **Answer:** **B. She said that she had finished her work.**

---

## Question 3

Choose the correct indirect speech.

**Direct:** He said, *"Do you know the answer?"*

A. He asked that I knew the answer.

B. He asked if I knew the answer.

C. He said if I know the answer.

D. He told if I knew the answer.

✅ **Answer:** **B. He asked if I knew the answer.**

---

## Question 4

Choose the correct indirect speech.

**Direct:** The teacher said, *"Study regularly."*

A. The teacher said to study regularly.

B. The teacher requested study regularly.

C. The teacher advised the students to study regularly.

D. The teacher asked study regularly.

✅ **Answer:** **C. The teacher advised the students to study regularly.**

---

## Question 5

Choose the correct indirect speech.

**Direct:** She said, *"Where do you live?"*

A. She asked where I lived.

B. She asked that where I lived.

C. She told where I live.

D. She said where I lived.

✅ **Answer:** **A. She asked where I lived.**

---

## Question 6

Choose the correct time-word change.

**Today** becomes:

A. Tomorrow

B. Yesterday

C. That day

D. This day

✅ **Answer:** **C. That day**

---

## Question 7

Choose the correct place-word change.

**Here** becomes:

A. There

B. This place

C. Near

D. Over there

✅ **Answer:** **A. There**

---

## Question 8

Choose the correct reporting verb.

A command is generally reported using:

A. asked

B. ordered

C. exclaimed

D. replied

✅ **Answer:** **B. ordered**

---

## Question 9

Choose the correct indirect speech.

**Direct:** He said, *"Hurray! We won the match."*

A. He said they won the match.

B. He exclaimed with joy that they had won the match.

C. He asked whether they won the match.

D. He replied that they won.

✅ **Answer:** **B. He exclaimed with joy that they had won the match.**

---

## Question 10

Quotation marks are removed in:

A. Direct Speech

B. Indirect Speech

# 💼 Direct & Indirect Speech in Professional Communication

Reported speech is widely used in meetings, emails, reports, and interviews.

---

## Meeting Example

**Direct**

The manager said, "Submit the report today."

**Indirect**

The manager instructed the team to submit the report that day.

---

## Email Example

**Direct**

The HR said, "Please upload your documents."

**Indirect**

The HR requested me to upload my documents.

---

## Interview Example

**Direct**

The interviewer asked, "Why do you want this job?"

**Indirect**

The interviewer asked why I wanted that job.

---

## News Reporting Example

**Direct**

The minister said, "We will launch a new policy."

**Indirect**

The minister announced that they would launch a new policy.

# ⚠️ Common Errors

### Error 1

❌ He asked that I was fine.

✅ He asked if I was fine.

---

### Error 2

❌ She told that she was busy.

✅ She said that she was busy.

OR

✅ She told me that she was busy.

---

### Error 3

❌ Today → Today

✅ Today → That day

---

### Error 4

❌ Here → Here

✅ Here → There

---

### Error 5

❌ Tomorrow → Tomorrow

✅ Tomorrow → The next day / The following day

---

# 📄 Direct & Indirect Speech Cheat Sheet

```text id="speechfinal"

DIRECT SPEECH

Uses quotation marks

Exact words


INDIRECT SPEECH

No quotation marks

Reports the meaning


Statements
↓

that


Yes/No Questions
↓

if / whether


WH Questions
↓

Keep WH word


Commands
↓

ordered to


Requests
↓

requested to


Advice
↓

advised to


Time Changes

Now → Then

Today → That day

Tomorrow → Next day

Yesterday → Previous day


Place Changes

Here → There

This → That

These → Those

```
