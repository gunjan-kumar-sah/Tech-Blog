# 📖 What is Tense?

A **Tense** is a grammatical concept that shows the **time of an action or event**.

In simple words:

> **Tense tells us when something happens.**

It tells whether an action happens:

* Present (Now)
* Past (Before)
* Future (Later)

---

# 🕒 Timeline of Tenses

```text id="tense-line"
Past              Present              Future

<----------------|-------------------->

Before           Now                  Later
```

---

# 🌍 Examples

### Present

> I play cricket.

(Action happening regularly)

---

### Past

> I played cricket.

(Action completed before)

---

### Future

> I will play cricket.

(Action will happen later)

---

# 🎯 Why Are Tenses Important?

Tenses help us:

* Speak correct English.
* Write professional emails.
* Communicate clearly.
* Understand time of actions.
* Clear grammar exams.

---

# 🧩 Types of Tenses

English has **three main tenses**:

| Tense         | Meaning                  |
| ------------- | ------------------------ |
| Present Tense | Action happening now     |
| Past Tense    | Action happened before   |
| Future Tense  | Action will happen later |

---

| ------------------ | ------------------ | ------------------ |

| Perfect Continuous | Perfect Continuous | Perfect Continuous |

# 1️⃣ Present Tense

## 📖 Definition

Present Tense describes:

* Actions happening now.
* Regular habits.
* General truths.
* Facts.

---

# Types of Present Tense

Present Tense has four forms:

1. Simple Present Tense
2. Present Continuous Tense
3. Present Perfect Tense
4. Present Perfect Continuous Tense

---

# 1. Simple Present Tense

## 📖 Definition

Simple Present Tense is used for:

* Daily habits
* Regular actions
* Universal truths
* Permanent situations

---

# Formula

### Positive Sentence

```text id="simplepresent01"
Subject + V1 + Object
```

For He/She/It:

```text id="simplepresent02"
Subject + V1(s/es) + Object
```

---

# Examples

### Example 1

> I play football.

I + play + football

---

### Example 2

> She plays football.

She + plays + football

---

### Example 3

> They study English.

They + study + English

---

# Negative Sentence

## Formula

```text id="simplepresent03"
Subject + do/does + not + V1 + Object
```

---

# Examples

✅ I do not play cricket.

✅ She does not like tea.

---

# Question Sentence

## Formula

```text id="simplepresent04"
Do/Does + Subject + V1 + Object?
```

---

# Examples

✅ Do you play football?

✅ Does he work here?

---

# ⚠️ Simple Present Rules

## Rule 1

He, She, It → Add **s/es**

Examples:

✅ He plays.

✅ She writes.

---

## Rule 2

I, We, You, They → Use V1

Examples:

✅ They play.

✅ We study.

---

# 🌍 Uses of Simple Present

## 1. Habit

Example:

> I wake up early.

---

## 2. Universal Truth

Example:

> The sun rises in the east.

---

## 3. Fact

Example:

> Water boils at 100°C.

---

# 2. Present Continuous Tense

## 📖 Definition

Present Continuous shows an action that is happening **right now**.

---

# Formula

```text id="presentcontinuous01"
Subject + am/is/are + V1+ing + Object
```

---

# Helping Verb Rules

| Subject     | Helping Verb |
| ----------- | ------------ |
| I           | am           |
| He/She/It   | is           |
| You/We/They | are          |

---

# Examples

✅ I am learning Python.

✅ She is reading a book.

✅ They are playing cricket.

---

# Negative Formula

```text id="presentcontinuous02"
Subject + am/is/are + not + V1+ing
```

Example:

> He is not working.

---

# Question Formula

```text id="presentcontinuous03"
Am/Is/Are + Subject + V1+ing?
```

Examples:

> Are you studying?

> Is she cooking?
