# 3. Present Perfect Tense

## 📖 Definition

Present Perfect Tense is used to describe:

* An action completed recently.
* An action that has a connection with the present.
* Life experiences.

---

# Formula

## Positive Sentence

```text
Subject + has/have + V3 + Object
```

---

# Helping Verb Rules

| Subject | Helping Verb |
| ------- | ------------ |
| I       | have         |
| We      | have         |
| You     | have         |
| They    | have         |
| He      | has          |
| She     | has          |
| It      | has          |

---

# Examples

### Example 1

> I have completed my project.

Completed → V3

---

### Example 2

> She has written a letter.

Written → V3

---

### Example 3

> They have finished their work.

Finished → V3

---

# Negative Sentence

## Formula

```text
Subject + has/have + not + V3 + Object
```

---

Examples:

✅ I have not completed the task.

✅ He has not arrived yet.

---

# Question Sentence

## Formula

```text
Has/Have + Subject + V3 + Object?
```

---

Examples:

✅ Have you finished your homework?

✅ Has she submitted the assignment?

---

# Uses of Present Perfect

## 1. Recently Completed Action

Example:

> I have just completed my work.

---

## 2. Experience

Example:

> I have visited Delhi.

---

## 3. Result in Present

Example:

> She has lost her keys.

---

# 4. Present Perfect Continuous Tense

## 📖 Definition

Present Perfect Continuous shows an action that:

* Started in the past.
* Is continuing until now.

---

# Formula

```text
Subject + has/have + been + V1+ing + Object + since/for
```

---

# Examples

### Example 1

> I have been studying for two hours.

---

### Example 2

> She has been working since morning.

---

### Example 3

> They have been playing for an hour.

---

# Since vs For

| Since          | For            |
| -------------- | -------------- |
| Starting point | Duration       |
| Since 2025     | For two years  |
| Since Monday   | For five hours |

---

# 🕒 Past Tense

## 📖 Definition

Past Tense describes an action that happened before now.

---

# Types of Past Tense

1. Simple Past Tense
2. Past Continuous Tense
3. Past Perfect Tense
4. Past Perfect Continuous Tense

---

# 5. Simple Past Tense

## 📖 Definition

Used for completed actions in the past.

---

# Formula

```text
Subject + V2 + Object
```

---

# Examples

✅ I played cricket yesterday.

✅ She visited Delhi last year.

✅ They completed the project.

---

# Negative Sentence

## Formula

```text
Subject + did not + V1 + Object
```

---

Examples:

✅ I did not go there.

✅ She did not study yesterday.

---

# Question Sentence

## Formula

```text
Did + Subject + V1 + Object?
```

---

Examples:

✅ Did you watch the movie?

✅ Did he complete the work?

---

# ⚠️ Important Rule

After **did**, always use **V1**.

Wrong:

❌ Did you went there?

Correct:

✅ Did you go there?

---

# 6. Past Continuous Tense

## 📖 Definition

Past Continuous describes an action that was happening at a specific time in the past.

---

# Formula

```text
Subject + was/were + V1+ing + Object
```

---

# Helping Verb Rules

| Subject     | Helping Verb |
| ----------- | ------------ |
| I           | was          |
| He/She/It   | was          |
| You/We/They | were         |

---

# Examples

✅ I was studying at night.

✅ She was cooking food.

✅ They were playing football.

---

# Negative Sentence

Formula:

```text
Subject + was/were + not + V1+ing
```

Examples:

✅ He was not sleeping.

✅ They were not working.

---

# Question Sentence

Formula:

```text
Was/Were + Subject + V1+ing?
```

Examples:

✅ Was he reading?

✅ Were they playing?

---

# 📌 Past Tense Quick Revision

```text id="pastquick"
Simple Past:

Subject + V2

Example:
I played.

Past Continuous:

Subject + was/were + V1+ing

Example:
I was playing.
```

---

# ⚠️ Common Errors

## Error 1

❌ I have went there.

✅ I have gone there.

---

## Error 2

❌ She did not went.

✅ She did not go.

---

## Error 3

❌ He was play cricket.

✅ He was playing cricket.
