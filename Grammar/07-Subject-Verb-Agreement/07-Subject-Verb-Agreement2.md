# 3️⃣ Advanced Subject-Verb Agreement Rules

Subject-Verb Agreement me kuch rules simple nahi hote. Placement exams aur interviews me mostly yahi rules test kiye jate hain.

---

# Rule 7: Either / Or & Neither / Nor Rule

## 📖 Definition

When two subjects are connected using:

* Either...or
* Neither...nor
* Not only...but also

The verb agrees with the **nearest subject**.

---

# Formula

```text id="either01"
Subject + or/nor + Subject + Verb
```

---

# Examples

### Example 1

✅ Either Rahul or his friends **are** coming.

Reason:

Nearest subject = friends (plural)

---

### Example 2

✅ Either his friends or Rahul **is** coming.

Reason:

Nearest subject = Rahul (singular)

---

### Example 3

✅ Neither the teacher nor the students **are** ready.

---

### Example 4

✅ Neither the students nor the teacher **is** ready.

---

# 🧠 Memory Trick

```text id="near01"
OR / NOR

Look at the nearest subject.
```

---

# Rule 8: Collective Noun Rule

## 📖 Definition

Collective nouns represent a group.

Examples:

* Team
* Family
* Class
* Committee
* Army

They usually take a singular verb when the group acts as one unit.

---

# Examples

✅ The team **is** winning the match.

(Team = One unit)

---

✅ The family **is** happy.

---

# When Collective Noun Takes Plural Verb

When members act separately.

Example:

✅ The team **are** arguing among themselves.

(Individual members)

---

# Rule 9: Words with "Each" and "Every"

## 📖 Rule

Each and Every always take singular verbs.

---

Examples:

✅ Each student **has** a book.

❌ Each student **have** a book.

---

✅ Every boy **plays** cricket.

❌ Every boy **play** cricket.

---

# Rule 10: Words Like "Everyone, Everybody"

These words are always singular.

Examples:

* Everyone
* Everybody
* Someone
* Somebody
* Nobody
* Anybody

---

Examples:

✅ Everyone **is** ready.

❌ Everyone **are** ready.

---

✅ Somebody **has** called you.

---

# Rule 11: Uncountable Nouns

Uncountable nouns take singular verbs.

Examples:

* Information
* Advice
* Knowledge
* Water
* Money
* Furniture

---

Examples:

✅ Information **is** important.

❌ Information **are** important.

---

✅ Money **is** necessary.

---

# Rule 12: Amount of Time, Money, Distance

When we talk about a total amount, use singular verb.

---

Examples:

✅ Ten kilometers **is** a long distance.

---

✅ Five hundred rupees **is** enough.

---

✅ Two years **is** a long time.

---

# Rule 13: "A Number of" vs "The Number of"

This is a very common exam rule.

---

## A number of

Meaning:

Many

Uses plural verb.

Example:

✅ A number of students **are** absent.

---

## The number of

Meaning:

Total count

Uses singular verb.

Example:

✅ The number of students **is** increasing.

---

# Rule 14: Titles of Books, Movies, Songs

Titles are considered singular.

---

Examples:

✅ "Harry Potter" **is** a famous book.

---

✅ "Friends" **is** my favorite series.

---

# Rule 15: There is / There are Rule

Verb depends on the noun after it.

---

Examples:

### Singular:

✅ There **is** a book on the table.

---

### Plural:

✅ There **are** books on the table.

---

# Rule 16: Some Nouns Look Plural But Are Singular

Some subjects end with "s" but take singular verbs.

Examples:

* Mathematics
* Physics
* Economics
* News

---

Examples:

✅ Mathematics **is** difficult.

❌ Mathematics **are** difficult.

---

✅ The news **is** true.

---

# Rule 17: Some Nouns Are Always Plural

Examples:

* Scissors
* Trousers
* Clothes
* Glasses

They take plural verbs.

---

Examples:

✅ My trousers **are** new.

✅ These scissors **are** sharp.

---

# 📊 Important Rule Summary Table

| Rule                    | Verb Usage      |
| ----------------------- | --------------- |
| He/She/It               | Singular Verb   |
| I/We/You/They           | Base Verb       |
| Each/Every              | Singular        |
| Everyone                | Singular        |
| A number of             | Plural          |
| The number of           | Singular        |
| Uncountable nouns       | Singular        |
| Two subjects with "and" | Plural          |
| Either/Neither          | Nearest Subject |

# ⚠️ Common Mistakes

## Mistake 1

❌ Everyone have a laptop.

✅ Everyone has a laptop.

---

## Mistake 2

❌ The students studies English.

✅ The students study English.

---

## Mistake 3

❌ Neither Rahul nor his friends is coming.

✅ Neither Rahul nor his friends are coming.

---

## Mistake 4

❌ The number of employees are increasing.

✅ The number of employees is increasing.
