# 📖 What is Grammar?

**Grammar** is the set of rules that tells us **how words are used to form correct sentences** in a language.

It helps us speak, write, read, and understand English correctly.

### Simple Definition

> **Grammar is the system of a language that helps us arrange words into meaningful and correct sentences.**

# 📚 Types of Grammar

There are two broad categories:

## 1️⃣ Descriptive Grammar

Describes how people actually use a language in everyday communication.

**Example:**

> "I'm gonna call you."

This is common in spoken English, even though it is informal.

---

## 2️⃣ Prescriptive Grammar

Provides formal rules for correct English, especially in writing and academic contexts.

**Example:**

> "I am going to call you."

This is the preferred form in formal writing.

# 📝 Basic Grammar Rules

### Rule 1: Every sentence begins with a capital letter.

✅ Correct:

> My name is Gunjan.

❌ Incorrect:

> my name is Gunjan.

---

### Rule 2: Every sentence ends with proper punctuation.

Examples:

* Statement → `.`
* Question → `?`
* Exclamation → `!`

✅ Correct:

> Where are you?

❌ Incorrect:

> Where are you

---

### Rule 3: A sentence must contain a subject and a verb.

✅ Correct:

> Rahul plays cricket.

❌ Incorrect:

> Rahul cricket.

---

### Rule 4: Use the correct tense.

✅ Correct:

> She is reading a book.

❌ Incorrect:

> She reading a book.

* ❌ Missing punctuation.
* ❌ Wrong verb forms.
* ❌ Incorrect subject–verb agreement.
* ❌ Mixing present and past tense.

# 🧠 Memory Tip

Remember the **3S Rule** for every sentence:

```text
Sentence = Subject + Verb + Sense
```

If a sentence has a **Subject**, a **Verb**, and makes complete **Sense**, it is usually grammatically complete.
