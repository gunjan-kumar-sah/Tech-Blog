# 📜 Code of Conduct

## 🤝 Our Commitment

We are committed to making participation in the **Tech-Blog** community a welcoming, respectful, and harassment-free experience for everyone.

Regardless of age, experience, education, nationality, gender identity, appearance, disability, ethnicity, religion, or technical background, every contributor deserves to be treated with respect.

Our goal is to create a positive learning environment where everyone can learn, share knowledge, and grow together.

---

# 🌟 Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive community include:

* 💬 Being respectful and kind.
* 🎓 Helping beginners learn.
* 🧠 Sharing knowledge openly.
* 🤝 Accepting constructive feedback gracefully.
* 📝 Writing clear and helpful documentation.
* 💡 Encouraging collaboration and innovation.
* 🌍 Respecting different viewpoints and experiences.
* ❤️ Supporting an inclusive learning environment.

---

# 🚫 Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are not acceptable:

* ❌ Harassment or discrimination of any kind.
* ❌ Personal attacks or insulting language.
* ❌ Hate speech or offensive content.
* ❌ Spam or promotional content unrelated to the project.
* ❌ Sharing copyrighted material without permission.
* ❌ Plagiarism or presenting someone else's work as your own.
* ❌ Intentionally misleading or harmful contributions.

---

# 📚 Content Guidelines

When contributing to **Tech-Blog**, please ensure that your content is:

* ✅ Accurate and factually correct.
* ✅ Original or properly attributed.
* ✅ Beginner-friendly.
* ✅ Well-structured and easy to understand.
* ✅ Written in clear Markdown formatting.
* ✅ Relevant to the repository's purpose.

---

# 🤝 Collaboration Guidelines

Please remember:

* Review existing content before creating new files.
* Keep discussions constructive and professional.
* Respect feedback from maintainers and contributors.
* Focus on improving the project rather than winning arguments.

---

# 🐛 Reporting Issues

If you notice behavior that violates this Code of Conduct:

1. Open a GitHub Issue (if appropriate).
2. Contact the maintainer with relevant details.
3. Provide links or screenshots if available.
4. Be respectful and factual when reporting concerns.

All reports will be reviewed fairly and handled with appropriate care.

---

# ⚖️ Enforcement

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing this Code of Conduct.

They may edit, remove, or reject contributions that do not align with these guidelines.

Repeated or serious violations may result in temporary or permanent restrictions from contributing to the project.
