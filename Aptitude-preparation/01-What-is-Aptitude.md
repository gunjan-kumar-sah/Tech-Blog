# 📖 Introduction

Aptitude is one of the most important skills evaluated in **campus placements, internships, competitive examinations, and job recruitment processes**. Whether you are preparing for companies like **TCS, Infosys, Wipro, Accenture, Cognizant, Capgemini, Deloitte, IBM, HCL, Amazon, or Google**, aptitude plays a significant role in the selection process.

Many students believe aptitude is only about solving mathematics problems. However, aptitude is much broader. It measures your **problem-solving ability, logical thinking, numerical skills, decision-making ability, and understanding of the English language**.

A strong aptitude score not only helps you clear online assessment rounds but also improves your confidence in solving real-world problems.

---

# 🤔 What is Aptitude?

**Aptitude** refers to a person's natural or acquired ability to learn, understand, and solve problems efficiently.

It is a combination of:

* Logical Thinking
* Numerical Ability
* Analytical Skills
* Decision Making
* Language Skills
* Problem Solving

In simple words,

> **Aptitude is your ability to solve problems quickly and accurately.**

---

# 🎯 Why is Aptitude Important?

Aptitude is used to evaluate how efficiently a candidate can analyze information and make decisions.

Companies use aptitude tests because they help identify candidates who can:

* Think logically
* Solve problems under pressure
* Work efficiently with numbers
* Analyze situations quickly
* Learn new technologies faster
* Make accurate decisions

Good aptitude skills are useful not only during placements but also in daily life, academics, programming, and software development.

# 🧩 Types of Aptitude

Aptitude is generally divided into three major sections.

## 1️⃣ Quantitative Aptitude

This section evaluates your mathematical and numerical ability.

### Common Topics

* Number System
* Percentage
* Ratio & Proportion
* Average
* Profit and Loss
* Simple Interest
* Compound Interest
* Time and Work
* Time, Speed and Distance
* Probability
* Permutation and Combination
* Data Interpretation

---

## 2️⃣ Logical Reasoning

Logical Reasoning measures your thinking and analytical skills.

### Common Topics

* Coding-Decoding
* Blood Relations
* Seating Arrangement
* Number Series
* Alphabet Series
* Direction Sense
* Puzzles
* Syllogism
* Statement and Conclusion

---

## 3️⃣ Verbal Ability
