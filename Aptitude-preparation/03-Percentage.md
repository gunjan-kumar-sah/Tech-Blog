# 🤔 What is Percentage?

A **percentage** means **"per hundred"**.

The symbol **%** represents "out of 100."

For example:

* 50% = 50 out of 100
* 25% = 25 out of 100
* 100% = Complete value

# 🧮 Basic Formula

**Percentage = (Part ÷ Whole) × 100**

Example:

A class has **40** students and **30** passed.

Percentage of students who passed:

= (30 ÷ 40) × 100

= 75%

---

# 📌 Finding Percentage of a Number

Example:

Find **25% of 240**

Solution:

25% = 25/100

= (25 × 240) / 100

= **60**

---

# 📈 Percentage Increase

Formula:

**Percentage Increase = ((New Value − Original Value) ÷ Original Value) × 100**

Example:

Salary increased from ₹20,000 to ₹24,000.

Increase = ₹4,000

Percentage Increase:

= (4000 ÷ 20000) × 100

= **20%**

---

# 📉 Percentage Decrease

Formula:

**Percentage Decrease = ((Original Value − New Value) ÷ Original Value) × 100**

Example:

Price decreased from ₹800 to ₹600.

Decrease = ₹200

Percentage Decrease:

= (200 ÷ 800) × 100

= **25%**

---

# ⚡ Shortcut Tricks

* 50% = Half
* 25% = One-fourth
* 20% = One-fifth
* 10% = Divide by 10
* 5% = Half of 10%
* 1% = Divide by 100

Example:

Find 5% of 600

10% = 60

5% = 30 ✅

# 📝 Solved Examples

### Example 1

Find 15% of 200.

Solution:

15% = 15/100

= (15 × 200) / 100

= **30**

---

### Example 2

A student scored **72 marks out of 90**.

Percentage:

= (72 ÷ 90) × 100

= **80%**

---

### Example 3

The price of a laptop increased from ₹50,000 to ₹55,000.

Percentage increase:

= (5000 ÷ 50000) × 100

= **10%**

* Using the wrong base value while calculating increase or decrease.
* Confusing percentages with decimals.
