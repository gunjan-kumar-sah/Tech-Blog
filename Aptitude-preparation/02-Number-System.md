# 📚 Types of Numbers

## 1️⃣ Natural Numbers (N)

Natural numbers start from **1** and continue infinitely.

```text
1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, ...
```

✔ Positive only

❌ Does not include Zero

---

## 2️⃣ Whole Numbers (W)

Whole numbers include **0** and all natural numbers.

```text
0,1,2,3,4,5...
```

---

## 3️⃣ Integers (Z)

Integers include:

* Negative Numbers
* Zero
* Positive Numbers

```text
... -3 -2 -1 0 1 2 3 ...
```

A prime number has **exactly two factors**:

## 8️⃣ Co-prime Numbers

Two numbers are called **co-prime** if their HCF is **1**.

# 🔥 Divisibility Rules

| Number | Rule                                                       |
| ------ | ---------------------------------------------------------- |
| 2      | Last digit is even                                         |
| 3      | Sum of digits divisible by 3                               |
| 4      | Last two digits divisible by 4                             |
| 5      | Last digit is 0 or 5                                       |
| 6      | Divisible by both 2 and 3                                  |
| 8      | Last three digits divisible by 8                           |
| 9      | Sum of digits divisible by 9                               |
| 10     | Last digit is 0                                            |
| 11     | Difference between alternate digit sums is divisible by 11 |

# 🔥 HCF (Highest Common Factor)

HCF is the greatest number that divides all given numbers exactly.

Example

```text
12 = 2 × 2 × 3

18 = 2 × 3 × 3

HCF = 2 × 3 = 6
```

---

# 🔥 LCM (Least Common Multiple)

LCM is the smallest number that is divisible by all given numbers.

Example

```text
12 = 2² × 3

18 = 2 × 3²

LCM = 2² × 3²

= 36
```

---

# 📌 Relation between HCF and LCM

For two numbers:

```text
HCF × LCM = Product of the Two Numbers
```

Example:

```text
Numbers = 12 and 18

HCF = 6

LCM = 36

6 × 36 = 216

12 × 18 = 216
```

✔ Formula Verified

# 📝 Solved Examples

### Example 1

Is **29** a prime number?

**Solution**

Factors:

```text
1
29
```

Only two factors.

**Answer:** Prime Number ✅

---

### Example 2

Find the HCF of **24 and 36**.

**Solution**

```text
24 = 2³ × 3

36 = 2² × 3²

HCF = 2² × 3

= 12
```

---

### Example 3

Find the LCM of **8 and 12**.

```text
8 = 2³

12 = 2² × 3

LCM = 2³ × 3

= 24
```
