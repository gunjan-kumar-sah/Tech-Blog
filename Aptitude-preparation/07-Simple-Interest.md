# 📖 Introduction

Whenever you borrow money from a bank or lend money to someone, interest is usually charged. One of the simplest methods of calculating this interest is called **Simple Interest (SI)**.

Simple Interest is a frequently asked topic in placement aptitude tests because it combines **basic mathematics, percentages, and logical calculations**. Once you understand the core formula, most questions become straightforward.

---

# 🌍 Real-Life Applications

Simple Interest is used in:

* 🏦 Bank loans
* 💳 Personal loans
* 🎓 Education loans
* 💰 Fixed deposits (basic cases)
* 🏢 Business lending
* 📈 Financial planning

---

# 🤔 What is Simple Interest?

**Simple Interest (SI)** is the interest calculated **only on the original Principal Amount** throughout the entire time period.

Unlike Compound Interest, the interest earned does **not** earn additional interest.

---

# 📌 Key Terms

### 💵 Principal (P)

The original amount borrowed or invested.

**Example:** ₹10,000

---

### 📈 Rate of Interest (R)

The percentage charged every year.

**Example:** 8% per annum

---

### ⏳ Time (T)

The duration for which the money is borrowed or invested.

**Example:** 3 years

---

### 💰 Simple Interest (SI)

The extra amount earned or paid after a certain period.

---

# 🧮 Formula

> **Note:** The interactive widget above illustrates growth using the standard interest model. For **Simple Interest**, we use the formula below because interest is calculated only on the original principal.

```text
Simple Interest = (Principal × Rate × Time) ÷ 100

SI = (P × R × T) / 100
```

---

# 💡 Total Amount Formula

```text
Amount = Principal + Simple Interest

A = P + SI
```

---

# 📝 Solved Example 1

A person deposits **₹5,000** in a bank at **10% per annum** for **2 years**.

### Given

* Principal = ₹5,000
* Rate = 10%
* Time = 2 years

### Solution

SI

= (5000 × 10 × 2) ÷ 100

= ₹1,000

Total Amount

= ₹5,000 + ₹1,000

= **₹6,000**

---

# 📝 Solved Example 2

Find the Simple Interest on **₹8,000** at **6% per annum** for **5 years**.

### Solution

SI

= (8000 × 6 × 5) ÷ 100

= ₹2,400

Total Amount

= ₹10,400

---

# ⚡ Shortcut Tricks

### ✅ Trick 1

Always write:

* P
* R
* T

before applying the formula.

---

### ✅ Trick 2

Cancel common factors before multiplying to save time.

Example:

```text
(5000 × 8 × 5) ÷ 100

↓

50 × 8 × 5

↓

2000
```

---

### ✅ Trick 3

If **Rate = 10%**, then yearly interest is simply **10% of Principal**.

Example:

Principal = ₹12,000

Interest per year = ₹1,200

For 4 years:

₹1,200 × 4 = ₹4,800

---

# 🎯 Key Takeaways

* Simple Interest is calculated only on the **Principal**.
* The Principal remains constant throughout the calculation.
* SI questions are generally easy and scoring in placement exams.
* Learn the formula thoroughly and practice mental calculations.
