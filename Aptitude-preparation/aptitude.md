# Aptitude Preparation Guide for Placements

> A complete beginner-friendly guide to Quantitative Aptitude, Logical Reasoning, and Verbal Ability for campus placements and competitive exams.

## 📌 Introduction

Aptitude is one of the most important skills required for campus placements, internships, government exams, and competitive recruitment processes. Companies such as TCS, Infosys, Wipro, Accenture, Cognizant, Capgemini, Deloitte, and many others assess candidates based on their aptitude skills before technical interviews.

This guide provides a structured roadmap to help beginners prepare efficiently.

---

# What is Aptitude?

Aptitude is the natural ability to solve problems, think logically, perform mathematical calculations, and understand language effectively.

It is generally divided into three sections:

* Quantitative Aptitude
* Logical Reasoning
* Verbal Ability (English)

---

# 1. Quantitative Aptitude

Quantitative Aptitude evaluates numerical and mathematical problem-solving skills.

### Important Topics

* Number System
* Simplification
* Percentage
* Ratio & Proportion
* Average
* Profit & Loss
* Simple Interest
* Compound Interest
* Time & Work
* Pipes & Cisterns
* Time, Speed & Distance
* Boats & Streams
* Partnership
* Mixture & Allegation
* HCF & LCM
* Ages
* Probability
* Permutation & Combination
* Data Interpretation

### Tips

* Learn formulas before solving questions.
* Practice calculations daily.
* Improve multiplication and division speed.
* Solve previous year questions regularly.

---

# 2. Logical Reasoning

Logical Reasoning measures analytical thinking and decision-making abilities.

### Important Topics

* Coding-Decoding
* Blood Relations
* Direction Sense
* Alphabet Series
* Number Series
* Seating Arrangement
* Puzzle
* Syllogism
* Statement & Conclusion
* Statement & Assumption
* Calendar
* Clock
* Data Sufficiency

### Tips

* Draw diagrams whenever required.
* Practice puzzles every day.
* Focus on understanding patterns instead of memorizing.

---

# 3. Verbal Ability

Verbal Ability tests English language proficiency.

### Important Topics

* Grammar
* Vocabulary
* Synonyms
* Antonyms
* Reading Comprehension
* Error Detection
* Fill in the Blanks
* Para Jumbles
* Sentence Improvement
* Active & Passive Voice
* Direct & Indirect Speech

### Tips

* Read English articles daily.
* Learn 10–15 new words every day.
* Practice grammar exercises regularly.
