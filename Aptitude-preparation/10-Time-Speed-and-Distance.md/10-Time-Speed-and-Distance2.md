# 🚆 Train Problems

Train questions are among the most frequently asked Time, Speed and Distance problems in placement exams.

## 📌 Key Rules

* When a train crosses a **pole**, it travels only its **own length**.
* When a train crosses a **platform**, it travels:

```text
Train Length + Platform Length
```

* When two trains move in:

  * **Same Direction** → Relative Speed = Difference of Speeds
  * **Opposite Direction** → Relative Speed = Sum of Speeds

---

# 🧮 Train Formulas

| Situation                       | Formula                                         |
| ------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------- |
| Crossing a Pole                 | Time = Train Length ÷ Speed                     |
| Crossing a Platform             | Time = (Train Length + Platform Length) ÷ Speed |
| Two Trains (Same Direction)     | Relative Speed = S₁ − S₂                        |
| Two Trains (Opposite Direction) | Relative Speed = S₁ + S₂                        |

---

## 📝 Example 1

A train of **180 m** crosses a pole in **9 seconds**.

### Solution

Speed

= 180 ÷ 9

= **20 m/s**

Convert to km/h:

20 × 18 ÷ 5 = **72 km/h**

✅ **Answer: 72 km/h**

---

## 📝 Example 2

A **200 m** train crosses a **300 m** platform in **25 seconds**.

Distance covered

= 200 + 300

= **500 m**

Speed

= 500 ÷ 25

= **20 m/s**

= **72 km/h**

# 🚤 Boats and Streams

Boats & Streams questions are based on the effect of water current.

## 📘 Important Terms

* **Still Water Speed (B):** Speed of the boat in calm water.
* **Stream Speed (S):** Speed of the flowing water.

---

## 🧮 Formulas

| Condition        | Formula |
| ---------------- | ------- |
| Downstream Speed | B + S   |
| Upstream Speed   | B − S   |

---

## 📝 Example

Boat Speed = **18 km/h**

Stream Speed = **4 km/h**

Downstream

= 18 + 4

= **22 km/h**

Upstream

= 18 − 4

= **14 km/h**

---

# 🏃 Relative Speed

Relative Speed is the speed at which two objects approach or move away from each other.

### Same Direction

```text
Relative Speed = Difference of Speeds
```

### Opposite Direction

```text
Relative Speed = Sum of Speeds
```

---

## 📝 Example

Car A

= 70 km/h

Car B

= 50 km/h

### Same Direction

Relative Speed

= 70 − 50

= **20 km/h**

### Opposite Direction

Relative Speed

= 70 + 50

= **120 km/h**

---

# ⚡ Shortcut Tricks

## 🚀 Trick 1

Memorize the conversion:

```text
18 km/h = 5 m/s
36 km/h = 10 m/s
54 km/h = 15 m/s
72 km/h = 20 m/s
90 km/h = 25 m/s
108 km/h = 30 m/s
```

These values appear very frequently in placement exams.

---

## 🚀 Trick 2

For train problems:

Always calculate **total distance** first.

Then apply:

```text
Speed = Distance ÷ Time
```

---

## 🚀 Trick 3

For average speed questions:

Do **not** average the speeds directly unless the distances and conditions make it valid.

Use:

```text
Average Speed = Total Distance ÷ Total Time
```

---

# 💼 Placement Tips

* Convert units before solving.
* Draw a small diagram for train or boat questions.
* Read carefully whether the motion is in the same or opposite direction.
* Keep track of units (m, km, s, h).

---

# ❌ Common Mistakes

* Forgetting to add the platform length.
* Mixing km/h with m/s.
* Using the wrong relative speed formula.
* Averaging speeds incorrectly.
